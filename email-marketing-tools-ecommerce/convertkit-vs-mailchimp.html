Overview: ConvertKit vs Mailchimp

ConvertKit and Mailchimp represent two different approaches to email marketing. ConvertKit, founded in 2013 by creator Nathan Barry, was built specifically for bloggers, podcasters, course creators, and authors. Its tagline—"Email marketing for creators"—reflects its focused mission. Mailchimp, founded in 2001, grew from a side project into a marketing platform for small businesses, later expanding into e-commerce, ads, and multi-channel marketing.

ConvertKit has become the go-to choice for creators who want simple automation, clean subscriber tagging, and commerce features built in. Mailchimp attracts businesses that need comprehensive marketing tools including A/B testing, advanced segmentation, social media ads, and e-commerce integrations.

The key difference: ConvertKit is designed for creators selling digital products—courses, ebooks, memberships. Mailchimp is designed for businesses doing e-commerce and multi-channel marketing. Your choice depends on whether you identify as a creator (ConvertKit) or a business (Mailchimp).

Creator-Focused Features

  • Built-in commerce: Sell digital products, courses, and memberships directly

  • Sponsor network: Connect with sponsors through ConvertKit's network

  • Creator community: Active community and educational resources

Mailchimp Business Strengths

  • Advanced templates: Hundreds of professionally designed email templates
  • Multi-channel marketing: Email, social ads, postcards, and landing pages
  • A/B testing: Robust split testing for subject lines, content, and send times
  • E-commerce integrations: Deep integration with Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento
  • Predictive analytics: AI-powered send time optimization and insights
  • Brand kit: Store brand assets and apply across campaigns

Feature verdict: ConvertKit excels at creator-specific features like digital product sales and simple automation. Mailchimp excels at business marketing features like templates, A/B testing, and multi-channel campaigns. Choose based on whether you're selling digital products as a creator (ConvertKit) or running e-commerce marketing (Mailchimp).

Automation Comparison

ConvertKit Automation

ConvertKit's automation is built for creators who want powerful sequences without complexity:

  • Visual builder: Drag-and-drop workflow builder
  • Triggers: Form submissions, tag additions, purchases, link clicks
  • Actions: Send emails, add tags, unsubscribe, delay, conditionals
  • Sequences: Evergreen email sequences for nurturing
  • Segments: Broadcast to tagged segments or entire list

Mailchimp Automation

Mailchimp's automation is more comprehensive but requires more setup:

  • Customer Journey: Visual journey builder with branching logic
  • Triggers: Signups, purchases, website activity, dates
  • Actions: Send emails, update contacts, target ads
  • Classic Automations: Welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase
  • Behavioral targeting: Target based on purchase history and engagement

Automation verdict: ConvertKit's automation is simpler to set up and ideal for creator workflows (course launches, newsletter sequences). Mailchimp's automation is more powerful for e-commerce (abandoned cart, purchase follow-up) but has a steeper learning curve.

Email Deliverability

ConvertKit Deliverability

  • Deliverability rate: 97-99% (industry leading)
  • Sender reputation: Strong focus on maintaining sender health

Mailchimp Deliverability

Deliverability verdict: Both platforms have excellent deliverability. ConvertKit has a slight edge for creator-focused sending patterns, while Mailchimp offers more tools for large-scale senders needing dedicated infrastructure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use ConvertKit for free forever?

Yes, ConvertKit's free plan supports up to 1,000 subscribers with unlimited emails. You get landing pages, sign-up forms, and basic automation. The free plan is genuinely useful for creators starting out. Upgrade to paid plans for advanced features and support.

Does ConvertKit have a website builder?

ConvertKit offers a basic website builder and landing page creator. It's suitable for simple creator sites and landing pages. For full-featured websites, most creators pair ConvertKit with WordPress, Squarespace, or another website platform.

Can I sell courses on Mailchimp?

Mailchimp integrates with course platforms like Teachable, Thinkific, and Kajabi. However, it doesn't have built-in course selling like ConvertKit. If you're selling courses directly from email, ConvertKit is more streamlined.

Which has better templates?

Mailchimp has far more templates—hundreds of professionally designed options for various industries and use cases. ConvertKit has simpler, fewer templates focused on creator needs. If design variety matters, Mailchimp wins. If you want clean, simple emails, ConvertKit's minimal templates are often enough.

Which platform is easier for beginners?

ConvertKit is generally easier for beginners because it has fewer features to learn and a cleaner interface. Mailchimp has more features, which can feel overwhelming. Both have good documentation and tutorials, but ConvertKit's creator-focused approach makes it more approachable.

Can I migrate from one to the other?

Yes, both platforms support CSV imports and exports. You can migrate subscriber lists, but you'll need to recreate automation sequences and email templates. Both platforms offer migration guides and support for paid accounts.

Final Verdict

🏆 Best for Creators: ConvertKit

ConvertKit wins for bloggers, podcasters, course creators, and anyone selling digital products. Its built-in commerce, simple automation, and generous free tier make it the best choice for creators. If you make money from content, courses, or digital downloads, ConvertKit is designed for you.

🏆 Best for E-commerce: Mailchimp

Mailchimp wins for e-commerce businesses and companies that need advanced marketing tools. Its templates, A/B testing, social ads integration, and analytics make it better suited for businesses running sophisticated marketing campaigns. If you're selling physical products or running multi-channel marketing, choose Mailchimp.

💡 Starting Out Recommendation

For beginners, try ConvertKit's free tier first. It's more generous (1,000 subscribers) and easier to learn. As you grow, reassess: if you need more templates or e-commerce features, consider Mailchimp. If you're building a creator business, ConvertKit scales well.

Our recommendation: If you're a creator, start with ConvertKit. If you're an e-commerce business, start with Mailchimp. Both offer free tiers, so you can test each platform before committing. Focus on which workflow feels more natural for your needs.
