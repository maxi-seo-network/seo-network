What is ConvertKit for E-commerce?

ConvertKit is an email marketing platform built specifically for creators—bloggers, YouTubers, podcasters, course creators, and influencers who sell products directly to their audience. Unlike traditional e-commerce email tools like Klaviyo, ConvertKit focuses on the creator economy with features like Creator Store (built-in digital product sales), commerce automation, and audience monetization tools.

For e-commerce businesses, ConvertKit works best for creator-focused brands selling digital products, online courses, memberships, or merchandise. The platform includes a native store builder that lets creators sell without setting up a separate e-commerce platform, making it unique in the email marketing space.

Quick verdict: ConvertKit excels for creator-entrepreneurs selling digital products, courses, or merchandise. The built-in Creator Store eliminates platform fees from third-party marketplaces. For traditional physical product stores, Klaviyo or ActiveCampaign may be better choices.

ConvertKit Creator Store: Built-in E-commerce

The Creator Store is ConvertKit's native e-commerce solution, allowing creators to sell digital products, paid newsletters, and merchandise without a separate platform:

What You Can Sell

  • Digital products: eBooks, templates, presets, guides, downloads
  • Online courses: Self-paced courses (integrates with Teachable)
  • Paid newsletters: Subscription-based email content
  • Merchandise: Physical products (integrates with print-on-demand)
  • Coaching/consulting: Bookable services with payment

How Creator Store Works

  1. Product creation: Upload your digital product (PDF, video, etc.)
  2. Pricing: Set your price (or pay-what-you-want)
  3. Checkout: ConvertKit handles the transaction and delivery
  4. Automation: Buyers are automatically added to your email list
  5. Delivery: Instant access to purchased products

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ConvertKit good for e-commerce?

ConvertKit works well for creator-focused e-commerce businesses selling digital products, courses, or merchandise. The Creator Store feature lets you sell directly without a separate platform. However, for traditional physical product stores, dedicated e-commerce tools like Klaviyo or Omnisend offer deeper integrations.

How much does ConvertKit cost for e-commerce?

ConvertKit offers a free plan for up to 1,000 subscribers. Paid plans start at $15/month (Creator) and FREE or $15/month (Creator Pro) for email marketing. The Creator Store has no monthly fee—you pay 3.5% + $0.30 per transaction for digital products.

ConvertKit vs Klaviyo for e-commerce: which is better?

Klaviyo is better for traditional e-commerce stores with deep Shopify integration and product-based automation. ConvertKit is better for creators selling digital products, courses, or building a personal brand. For physical products, choose Klaviyo. For digital products and courses, ConvertKit excels.

What is ConvertKit Creator Store?

Creator Store is ConvertKit's built-in e-commerce feature that lets creators sell digital products, paid newsletters, and merchandise directly from their ConvertKit account. There's no monthly fee—you only pay 3.5% + $0.30 per transaction. Buyers are automatically added to your email list.

Can I use ConvertKit with Shopify?

Yes, ConvertKit has a Shopify integration via the official app. It syncs customer data and order events for automation triggers. This is useful for creators who sell both digital products (via Creator Store) and physical products (via Shopify).

How does ConvertKit compare to Gumroad?

Both ConvertKit and Gumroad let creators sell digital products. ConvertKit charges 3.5% + $0.30 per transaction, while Gumroad charges a 10% fee on sales. ConvertKit also includes email marketing built-in, while Gumroad is primarily a selling platform. For creators who need both email and selling, ConvertKit is more cost-effective.
