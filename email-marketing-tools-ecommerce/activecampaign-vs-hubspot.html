ActiveCampaign vs HubSpot: Complete Email Marketing & CRM Comparison (2026)

Published: March 1, 2026 | Updated: March 18, 2026 | Tools Reviews Hub Team

ActiveCampaign vs HubSpot:

📋 Quick Verdict

  • Choose ActiveCampaign if: You want best-in-class email automation at an affordable price
  • Choose HubSpot if: You need an all-in-one platform combining CRM, marketing, sales, and content
  • Starting price: ActiveCampaign $29/mo (1,000 contacts); HubSpot $15/mo (1,000 contacts)
  • Free plans: ActiveCampaign has no free plan; HubSpot has generous free CRM and tools
  • Best for: ActiveCampaign = email-centric businesses; HubSpot = inbound marketing teams

Overview: ActiveCampaign vs HubSpot

ActiveCampaign and HubSpot represent two different philosophies in the marketing technology space. ActiveCampaign, founded in 2003, focuses on delivering the best possible email marketing automation with sophisticated workflow builders and deep segmentation capabilities. HubSpot, founded in 2006, pioneered the inbound marketing methodology and built an all-in-one platform that combines CRM, marketing automation, sales tools, content management, and customer service.

ActiveCampaign has become the go-to choice for businesses that want powerful automation without the complexity (and cost) of enterprise platforms. HubSpot attracts companies that want everything in one place—a single source of truth for marketing, sales, and service data.

The key difference: ActiveCampaign excels at email automation depth—it's a specialist tool. HubSpot excels at platform breadth—it's a generalist tool. Choose based on whether you need deep email capabilities (ActiveCampaign) or broad platform features (HubSpot).

Email Automation Comparison

ActiveCampaign Automation Strengths

  • Visual workflow builder: Drag-and-drop automation with complex branching logic
  • Event tracking: Track website actions and trigger sophisticated sequences
  • Split actions: Test different paths within a single automation
  • Goal tracking: Automatically adjust journeys based on conversions
  • Dynamic content: Personalize emails based on contact data
  • Site tracking: Trigger automations based on page visits

HubSpot Automation Strengths

  • Workflow templates: Pre-built automation for common use cases
  • CRM integration: Automation tied to sales pipeline stages
  • Lead scoring: Sophisticated scoring across marketing and sales
  • Smart content: Dynamic website content based on contact lists
  • Multi-channel: Automate emails, ads, and web push together
  • Object-based workflows: Automate deals, tickets, and custom objects

Automation verdict: ActiveCampaign's workflow builder is more intuitive and powerful for pure email automation. HubSpot's strength is automating across the entire customer lifecycle—including sales and service. For email-centric automation, choose ActiveCampaign. For cross-department automation, choose HubSpot.

Migration Considerations

Migrating to ActiveCampaign

  1. Export contacts from your current platform (CSV format)
  2. Map custom fields to ActiveCampaign fields
  3. Import contacts and assign to lists/tags
  4. Recreate automations using ActiveCampaign's workflow builder
  5. Set up integrations with your existing tools
  6. Test thoroughly before sending to your full list

Migration time: 1-3 weeks depending on automation complexity

Migrating to HubSpot

  1. Use HubSpot's migration tools or import contacts via CSV
  2. Map properties to HubSpot's default and custom properties
  3. Set up marketing emails and templates
  4. Create workflows for automation (use templates to speed up)
  5. Connect your website with tracking code
  6. Configure sales pipeline if using CRM features

Migration time: 2-6 weeks depending on complexity and training needs

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use both ActiveCampaign and HubSpot together?

Yes, many businesses use ActiveCampaign for email automation while using HubSpot's free CRM for sales pipeline management. You can sync contacts between platforms using Zapier or native integration. However, this adds complexity and cost.

Does ActiveCampaign have a CRM?

ActiveCampaign includes a basic CRM in its Plus tier and above. It has deal pipelines, contact management, and basic sales automation. However, it's not as comprehensive as HubSpot's CRM, which includes ticketing, custom objects, and advanced reporting.

Which has better email deliverability?

Both platforms have excellent deliverability (97%+). ActiveCampaign is known for particularly strong deliverability with dedicated IP options for high-volume senders. HubSpot has improved significantly and maintains good sending reputation.

Can I start with HubSpot free and move to ActiveCampaign later?

Yes, but it's not seamless. You can export contacts from HubSpot and import to ActiveCampaign. However, you'll lose automation workflows, email templates, and historical data. Plan your migration carefully if you expect to switch platforms.

Which platform is better for agencies?

Both work well for agencies. ActiveCampaign offers agency features including client management and white-label options in certain tiers. HubSpot's Solutions Partner program provides certifications, resources, and revenue opportunities. Many agencies use ActiveCampaign for email-centric clients and HubSpot for clients needing full-stack marketing.

How does pricing scale with list size?

ActiveCampaign pricing is based primarily on contact count. As your list grows, you'll pay more. HubSpot's free and starter tiers have contact limits, but Professional and Enterprise tiers include a set number of contacts (2,000 and 10,000 respectively) with additional contacts costing extra. At scale, ActiveCampaign is typically much more affordable.

Final Verdict

🏆 Best for Email Marketing: ActiveCampaign

ActiveCampaign wins for businesses focused primarily on email marketing. Its workflow builder is more intuitive, segmentation is more powerful, and pricing scales more reasonably. If you need the best email automation platform and have your CRM handled elsewhere, ActiveCampaign is the clear choice.

🏆 Best for All-in-One Platform: HubSpot

HubSpot wins for businesses that want everything in one place. The integrated CRM, content management, marketing automation, and sales tools create a unified platform. If you're building an inbound marketing strategy and can invest in a comprehensive system, HubSpot delivers.

💡 Budget Consideration

For businesses with 5,000+ contacts needing automation, ActiveCampaign typically costs 70-90% less than HubSpot's Professional tier. The gap widens as your list grows. However, HubSpot's free tier is genuinely useful for small businesses just starting out.

Our recommendation: If email marketing is your priority and you're budget-conscious, start with ActiveCampaign's free trial. If you need a complete growth platform and can invest in the Professional tier, HubSpot offers more comprehensive features. Test both platforms with your actual workflows before committing.
