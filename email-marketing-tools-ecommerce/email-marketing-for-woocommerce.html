How to Set Up Klaviyo for WooCommerce (Step-by-Step)

Klaviyo is the most popular email marketing platform for WooCommerce. Here's how to set it up:

Step 1: Install the Klaviyo Plugin

  1. Go to WordPress Admin → Plugins → Add New
  2. Search for "Klaviyo"
  3. Install and activate the official Klaviyo plugin
  4. Go to WooCommerce → Settings → Integration → Klaviyo

Step 2: Connect Your Klaviyo Account

  1. Create a Klaviyo account (if you don't have one)
  2. Go to Klaviyo → Settings → API Keys
  3. Create a new API key with full permissions
  4. Copy the API key
  5. Paste it into the Klaviyo plugin settings in WordPress
  6. Click "Connect"

Step 3: Enable Data Sync

  1. In Klaviyo, go to Integrations → WooCommerce
  2. Enable sync for:
    • ✓ Customers
    • ✓ Orders
    • ✓ Products
    • ✓ Categories
  3. Enable the "Subscribe to newsletter" checkbox on checkout
  4. Wait 24-48 hours for full historical data sync

Step 4: Set Up Abandoned Cart Flow

  1. In Klaviyo, go to Flows → Create Flow
  2. Select "Metric" → "Added to Cart"
  3. Add filter: "Placed Order zero times since starting this flow"
  4. Add time delay: 1 hour
  5. Add email template with product image and cart link
  6. Add second email at 24 hours
  7. Add third email at 72 hours with discount offer
  8. Activate flow

Step 5: Add Signup Forms

  1. In Klaviyo, go to Signup Forms → Create New
  2. Choose popup, flyout, or embedded form
  3. Customize design and targeting
  4. Klaviyo provides a JavaScript snippet—add it to your WordPress header
  5. Or use the Klaviyo Gutenberg block

Setup time: 1-2 hours for basic setup

How to Set Up AutomateWoo for WooCommerce

AutomateWoo is a premium WooCommerce plugin that runs entirely on your WordPress site. Here's how to set it up:

Step 1: Purchase and Install AutomateWoo

  1. Purchase AutomateWoo from WooCommerce.com ($199/year)
  2. Download the plugin ZIP file
  3. Go to WordPress Admin → Plugins → Add New → Upload Plugin
  4. Upload and activate AutomateWoo
  5. Enter your license key in WooCommerce → Settings → AutomateWoo

Step 2: Set Up Email Settings

  1. Go to AutomateWoo → Settings → Email
  2. Configure sender name and email
  3. Set up email template colors and logo
  4. Choose whether to use WordPress email or connect an SMTP service

Step 3: Create Your First Workflow

  1. Go to AutomateWoo → Workflows → Add New
  2. Choose trigger: "Cart Abandoned"
  3. Set timing: "Wait 1 hour after cart abandoned"
  4. Add action: "Send Email"
  5. Customize email template (use variables like {{customer.first_name}}, {{cart.url}})
  6. Add second rule: Wait 24 hours, send follow-up
  7. Activate workflow

Step 4: Pre-Built Workflows

AutomateWoo includes pre-built workflows you can import:

  • Abandoned Cart Recovery
  • Welcome Series
  • Post-Purchase Follow-up
  • Product Review Request
  • Win-back for Inactive Customers
  • Birthday Email
  • VIP Customer Reward

Setup time: 1-2 hours for basic setup

Essential Email Flows for WooCommerce Stores

Set up these five automated flows to generate revenue on autopilot:

1. Welcome Series

Trigger: New customer registration or newsletter signup

Emails: 3-5 emails over 7 days

Revenue Impact: 3-5x higher conversion than one-time campaigns

Recommended Structure:

  • Email 1 (Immediate): Welcome + 10-15% discount for first order
  • Email 2 (Day 2): Brand story + featured products
  • Email 3 (Day 4): Customer reviews and social proof
  • Email 4 (Day 7): Last chance for discount offer

2. Abandoned Cart Recovery

Trigger: Cart created, no purchase in 1 hour

Emails: 3 emails over 72 hours

Revenue Impact: Recovers 10-15% of lost sales

Recommended Structure:

  • Email 1 (1-2 hours): "You left something behind" - friendly reminder
  • Email 2 (24 hours): Social proof + product benefits
  • Email 3 (48-72 hours): 10-15% discount incentive

3. Post-Purchase Series

Trigger: Order completed

Emails: 3-4 emails

Revenue Impact: Increases repeat purchases by 20-30%

Recommended Structure:

  • Email 1 (Immediate): Order confirmation
  • Email 2 (7 days): Product tips + cross-sell related products
  • Email 3 (14-21 days): Review request
  • Email 4 (30 days): Replenishment reminder (for consumables)

4. Win-Back Campaign

Trigger: No purchase in 60-90 days

Emails: 2-3 emails

Revenue Impact: Reactivates 5-10% of dormant customers

5. Customer Birthday

Trigger: Customer birthday (if collected)

Emails: 1 email

Revenue Impact: Birthday emails have 3x higher open rates

Optimizing WooCommerce Email Settings

WooCommerce sends transactional emails by default. Here's how to optimize them:

Enable WooCommerce Transactional Emails

  1. Go to WooCommerce → Settings → Emails
  2. Enable all email types:
    • New Order
    • Cancelled Order
    • Failed Order
    • Order On-Hold
    • Processing Order
    • Completed Order
    • Customer Invoice
    • Customer Note
    • Reset Password
    • New Account
  3. Customize sender name and email address

Customize Email Templates

  1. Go to WooCommerce → Settings → Emails → Email Templates
  2. Upload your logo
  3. Customize colors to match your brand
  4. Add custom footer text
  5. Preview all templates before saving

Improve Email Deliverability

WordPress default email (PHP mail) often goes to spam. Use a proper email service:

  • WP Mail SMTP: Connect to Gmail, Outlook, or SMTP provider
  • SendGrid: Free tier for 100 emails/day
  • Mailgun: Free tier for 5,000 emails/month
  • Amazon SES: Very cheap for high volume ($0.10 per 1,000 emails)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best email marketing plugin for WooCommerce?

Klaviyo is the best email marketing plugin for WooCommerce due to its official integration, real-time sync, and e-commerce automation features. Mailchimp and ActiveCampaign are strong alternatives. WooCommerce Email (AutomateWoo) is ideal for stores wanting a native WordPress solution.

Does WooCommerce have built-in email marketing?

WooCommerce includes basic transactional emails (order confirmation, shipping, etc.) but doesn't have built-in marketing email capabilities. For marketing automation, you need a plugin like Klaviyo, Mailchimp, or AutomateWoo. WooCommerce Email is a paid extension that adds marketing features.

How do I connect Klaviyo to WooCommerce?

To connect Klaviyo to WooCommerce: 1) Install the official Klaviyo plugin from WordPress.org, 2) Enter your Klaviyo API key in the plugin settings, 3) Enable the data you want to sync (orders, products, customers), 4) Enable the Klaviyo signup checkbox on checkout, 5) Wait 24-48 hours for full historical data sync.

How much does email marketing cost for WooCommerce stores?

Email marketing for WooCommerce typically costs $0-100/month for small stores (0-2,500 contacts), $100-200/month for medium stores (2,500-10,000 contacts), and $200-500/month for large stores. AutomateWoo ($199/year) has no per-contact fees, making it cost-effective for large lists.

What email automation is essential for WooCommerce stores?

Essential email automation for WooCommerce stores includes: 1) Welcome series for new subscribers, 2) Abandoned cart recovery (10-15% recovery rate), 3) Post-purchase follow-up, 4) Customer win-back for inactive buyers, 5) Product review requests. These automated flows generate 15-30% of e-commerce revenue.
