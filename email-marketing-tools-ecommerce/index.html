Why Email Marketing Matters for E-commerce

Email marketing delivers the highest ROI of any marketing channel for e-commerce stores—averaging $36 return for every $1 spent. Unlike social media, you own your email list and can reach customers directly without algorithm changes affecting your reach.

For e-commerce specifically, email marketing powers:

  • Abandoned cart recovery - Recover 10-15% of lost sales
  • Welcome series - Convert new subscribers at 3-5x higher rate
  • Post-purchase follow-ups - Drive repeat purchases and reviews
  • Win-back campaigns - Reactivate dormant customers
  • VIP programs - Reward your highest-value customers

But with dozens of platforms claiming to be "best for e-commerce," how do you choose? This guide breaks down every major email marketing tool for online stores.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best email marketing tool for Shopify stores?

Klaviyo is widely considered the best email marketing tool for Shopify stores due to its deep native integration, product recommendation engine, and built-in abandoned cart recovery. ActiveCampaign is a strong alternative if you need CRM features alongside email marketing.

How much does email marketing cost for e-commerce?

Email marketing for e-commerce typically costs $0-60/month for up to 1,000 contacts (many providers offer free tiers), $50-150/month for 5,000 contacts, and $150-300/month for 10,000+ contacts. Pricing varies by platform and features needed.

Is Mailchimp good for e-commerce?

Mailchimp is good for small e-commerce stores just starting out, thanks to its free plan and ease of use. However, for stores serious about email-driven revenue, Klaviyo and ActiveCampaign offer more advanced e-commerce features like sophisticated abandoned cart flows, product recommendations, and revenue attribution.

Do I need SMS marketing for my e-commerce store?

SMS marketing is highly effective for e-commerce, with open rates of 90%+ compared to 20% for email. Consider adding SMS if you have: order confirmation needs, time-sensitive promotions, or customers who prefer text communication. Klaviyo and ActiveCampaign both offer SMS as an add-on feature.

Email Marketing FAQ

How often should I email my list?

For e-commerce, 2-4 emails per week is optimal for engaged subscribers. Reduce to 1-2 per week for less engaged segments. Always segment by engagement level to avoid overwhelming inactive subscribers.

What's the ideal email length?

E-commerce emails perform best at 50-150 words for promotional emails, 200-300 words for educational content. Keep abandoned cart emails under 100 words with a clear CTA.

Should I buy email lists?

Never. Bought lists damage deliverability, violate CAN-SPAM and GDPR, and have poor engagement. Focus on organic growth through popups, checkout opt-ins, and lead magnets.

What's a good open rate for e-commerce?

Average open rate is 15-25%. Good is 25-35%. Excellent is 40%+. Rates vary by industry: fashion 20-30%, electronics 15-25%, health & beauty 25-35%.

How do I reduce unsubscribe rates?

Segment by interest, reduce frequency for low engagers, A/B test content relevance, offer preference center for subscribers to choose topics and frequency, remove inactive subscribers proactively.

Email Compliance & Regulations

CAN-SPAM Act Requirements

  • Include physical mailing address in every email
  • Provide clear unsubscribe link (must work within 10 days)
  • Use accurate subject lines (no misleading content)
  • Identify messages as advertisements when applicable

GDPR Requirements (EU Subscribers)

  • Explicit opt-in consent required (no pre-checked boxes)
  • Easy unsubscribe in every email
  • Double opt-in recommended
  • Store consent records

CASL Requirements (Canada)

  • Express or implied consent required
  • Include business name and contact information
  • Unsubscribe must work within 10 business days
