Abandoned Cart Email Templates That Convert (2026 Examples)

Last updated: March 2026 | 15+ templates included

📋 Key Takeaways

  • Recovery Rate: 10-15% of abandoned carts can be recovered with email sequences
  • Optimal Timing: Email 1 at 1-2 hours, Email 2 at 24 hours, Email 3 at 48-72 hours

  • Discount Strategy: Save discounts for the 3rd email to avoid training customers to abandon

Why Abandoned Cart Emails Matter

With an average cart abandonment rate of 70%, most e-commerce stores lose the majority of potential sales at checkout. But here's the good news: abandoned cart emails recover 10-15% of lost sales on average—that's revenue you're leaving on the table without automation.

Abandoned Cart Email Subject Lines That Work

Your subject line determines whether your email gets opened. Here are subject lines that consistently perform:

Simple & Direct (Best for First Email)

Subject Line Avg. Open Rate Best For
"You left something behind" 45-55% All stores
"Complete your order" 42-50% Direct brands
"Your cart is waiting" 40-48% Friendly brands
"Still thinking about it?" 38-46% Conversational tone

Personalized Subject Lines

Adding product names or customer names increases open rates by 20-30%:

  • "[Product Name] is still in your cart"
  • "[Name], complete your order today"
  • "Your [Product Name] is waiting"
  • "[Name] - did you forget something?"

Urgency-Based Subject Lines (Best for 2nd/3rd Email)

Subject Line When to Use Risk Level
"Your cart expires soon" 24-48 hours after abandonment Low
"Only a few left in stock!" Limited inventory items Medium (must be true)
"Your discount expires in 24 hours" With discount offer Low
"Price drop on your item" If price actually dropped Low (must be true)

⚠️ Warning: Only use urgency-based subject lines when genuine. Fake scarcity damages trust and hurts deliverability.

Email 1: The Friendly Reminder (1-2 Hours After Abandonment)

Send within 1-2 hours while the purchase intent is still fresh. Keep it simple—no discount yet.

Subject Line Options

  • "You left something behind 👀"
  • "Your cart is waiting for you"
  • "Did you forget something?"

Template

Hi [First Name],

We noticed you left some items in your cart at [Store Name]. We've saved them for you—complete your order before they sell out!

[PRODUCT IMAGE]

[Product Name] - $XX.XX

Complete Your Order →

Questions about your order? Reply to this email—we're here to help.

Cheers,
The [Store Name] Team

Why This Works

  • Timing: Sent within hours while intent is high
  • Simplicity: No discount trains customers to wait
  • Clear CTA: One obvious action to take
  • Product image: Visual reminder of what they wanted

Expected metrics: 45-55% open rate, 8-12% click rate, 3-5% conversion rate

Email 3: The Discount Incentive (48-72 Hours After Abandonment)

At 48-72 hours, offer a discount to convert hesitant buyers. This is your last chance to recover the cart.

Subject Line Options

  • "Here's 10% off your order 🎁"
  • "A special offer just for you"
  • "Your cart + a gift from us"

Template

Hi [First Name],

We'd love to see you complete your order. Here's a little something to help:

Get 10% OFF your order

Use code: COMEBACK10

[PRODUCT IMAGE]

Claim My 10% Off →

Offer expires in 48 hours. Can't be combined with other discounts.

We hope to see you soon!

Cheers,
The [Store Name] Team

Discount Strategy Tips

  • 10-15% is optimal—enough to motivate without killing margins
  • Free shipping often works better than percentage off for lower-priced items
  • Limited time creates urgency (48-72 hours is ideal)
  • Track coupon usage to measure recovery rate

Why This Works

  • Incentive: Final push for price-sensitive buyers
  • Urgency: Expiration motivates action
  • Exclusivity: "Just for you" makes it feel personal
  • Clear CTA: Single button to redeem

Expected metrics: 30-40% open rate, 8-15% click rate, 5-10% conversion rate

Optimal Timing Strategy

When to send each email depends on your product type:

Standard Timing (Most Products)

  • Email 1: 1-2 hours after abandonment
  • Email 2: 24 hours after abandonment
  • Email 3: 48-72 hours after abandonment

Fast-Timing (Fashion, Consumables)

  • Email 1: 30 minutes after abandonment
  • Email 2: 12 hours after abandonment
  • Email 3: 24 hours after abandonment

Slow-Timing (Luxury, High-Ticket)

  • Email 1: 4-6 hours after abandonment
  • Email 2: 48 hours after abandonment
  • Email 3: 5-7 days after abandonment

Pro Tip: Test your timing. Your analytics will show when your customers typically convert. Adjust accordingly.

How to Set Up Abandoned Cart Emails

Klaviyo Setup

  1. Navigate to Flows → Create Flow → Metric
  2. Select "Added to Cart" as trigger
  3. Add filter: "Placed Order zero times since this flow"
  4. Add time delay: 1 hour
  5. Add email template (use templates above)
  6. Add conditional: If not placed order, send Email 2 (24 hours)
  7. Add conditional: If not placed order, send Email 3 (72 hours)
  8. Preview, test, and activate

→ Full Klaviyo Setup Guide

ActiveCampaign Setup

  1. Go to Automations → New Automation
  2. Select "E-commerce: Abandoned Cart" template
  3. Customize trigger conditions (cart value, time delay)
  4. Edit email templates with your content
  5. Add conditions to skip if order placed
  6. Test with preview contacts
  7. Activate automation

→ Full ActiveCampaign Setup Guide

Mailchimp Setup

  1. Connect e-commerce store (Shopify/WooCommerce app)
  2. Go to Campaigns → Email → Automated
  3. Select "Abandoned Cart" automation
  4. Customize timing (default is 1 hour, 24 hours, 48 hours)
  5. Edit email templates
  6. Preview and start automation

Abandoned Cart Email Best Practices

Do's

  • Include product images—visual reminders convert 2x better
  • Show product price and cart total—reduce friction
  • Make the CTA button obvious—"Complete Order" should be prominent
  • Link directly to cart—don't send to homepage
  • Add customer support contact—reduce purchase anxiety
  • Optimize for mobile—60%+ of carts are abandoned on mobile
  • A/B test subject lines—small improvements compound

Don'ts

  • Don't discount immediately—trains customers to wait
  • Don't send more than 3 emails—becomes spammy
  • Don't use fake urgency—"cart expires" when it doesn't hurts trust
  • Don't send to bots/spam emails—filter by valid emails
  • Don't include too many products—max 5 items shown
  • Don't send to repeat abandoners—suppress if they abandon frequently

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an abandoned cart email?

An abandoned cart email is an automated message sent to customers who added items to their shopping cart but didn't complete the purchase. These emails remind customers of their abandoned items and typically include product images, cart totals, and often a discount or incentive to encourage purchase completion.

What percentage of abandoned carts can be recovered?

Typically 10-15% of abandoned carts can be recovered through email sequences. The first email recovers 5-8%, second email adds 3-5%, and third email (often with a discount) adds 2-3%. Stores with optimized sequences see up to 20% recovery rates.

When should I send abandoned cart emails?

Send abandoned cart emails at these intervals: Email 1 at 1-2 hours after abandonment (friendly reminder), Email 2 at 24 hours (urgency/social proof), Email 3 at 48-72 hours (discount incentive). Timing depends on your product type—fashion works faster, luxury needs longer.

Should I offer a discount in abandoned cart emails?

Offer discounts strategically. First email: No discount (focus on reminder). Second email: Consider free shipping or small incentive. Third email: 10-15% discount if still unpurchased. Discounting immediately trains customers to abandon for discounts. Reserve discounts for the final recovery attempt.

What subject lines work best for abandoned cart emails?

Best-performing abandoned cart subject lines include: "You left something behind" (simple, effective), "Complete your order" (direct), "Your cart is waiting" (friendly reminder), "Still thinking about it?" (conversational), "Your cart expires soon" (urgency). Personalization (adding product name) increases open rates by 20-30%.
