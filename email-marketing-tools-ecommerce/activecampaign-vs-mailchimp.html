Overview: ActiveCampaign vs Mailchimp

ActiveCampaign and Mailchimp represent two different approaches to email marketing. Mailchimp, founded in 2001, pioneered accessible email marketing for small businesses with its drag-and-drop builder and forever-free plan. ActiveCampaign, launched in 2003, focused from the start on marketing automation and CRM integration, making it the choice for businesses ready to graduate from basic email blasts.

Today, Mailchimp serves over 13 million users worldwide and is known for its beginner-friendly interface and generous free plan. ActiveCampaign has built a reputation for powerful automation capabilities and a built-in CRM, serving businesses that need sophisticated customer journey mapping.

The key difference: Mailchimp prioritizes ease of use and accessibility; ActiveCampaign prioritizes automation depth and CRM integration. Your choice depends on whether you need simplicity or power.

Automation Capabilities

ActiveCampaign Automation

ActiveCampaign's automation builder is its crown jewel. The visual workflow editor supports:

  • Multi-branch journeys: Split paths based on any condition
  • Goal tracking: Automatically remove contacts from sequences when they convert
  • Wait until conditions: Pause until specific actions occur
  • Site tracking triggers: Fire automations based on page visits
  • Event tracking: Custom events from your app or website
  • Split testing: Test automation paths against each other
  • Dynamic content: Show different content to different segments

Mailchimp Automation

Mailchimp's automation is more basic but covers essential use cases:

  • Welcome series: Pre-built templates for new subscribers
  • Abandoned cart: E-commerce recovery emails
  • Customer re-engagement: Win-back campaigns
  • Date-based: Birthday and anniversary emails
  • Post-purchase: Follow-up sequences

Our take: If you need complex multi-step journeys with conditional branching, ActiveCampaign is significantly more capable. For simple welcome series and abandoned cart emails, Mailchimp is sufficient and easier to set up.

CRM Comparison

ActiveCampaign CRM

ActiveCampaign includes a full CRM at no extra cost on Plus plans and above:

  • Contact and deal management
  • Pipeline tracking with visual boards
  • Lead scoring based on engagement
  • Automated deal assignment
  • Sales automation workflows
  • Email integration (send from CRM)
  • Mobile app for sales teams

Mailchimp CRM Features

Mailchimp has basic contact management but no true CRM:

  • Contact profiles with engagement history
  • Basic segmentation
  • Tags and audience groups
  • No deal tracking
  • No pipeline management
  • No lead scoring

Winner: ActiveCampaign, by a wide margin. If you need sales team tools alongside marketing, ActiveCampaign eliminates the need for a separate CRM.

When to Choose Each Platform

Choose ActiveCampaign When:

  • You need advanced automation workflows with branching logic
  • You want email marketing and CRM in one platform
  • You're running complex customer journeys (B2B or B2C)
  • You need to track website behavior and trigger automations
  • You want lead scoring for sales teams
  • You're integrating with custom apps via API
  • You need split testing within automation flows

Choose Mailchimp When:

  • You're just starting with email marketing
  • You want a free plan for testing or small lists
  • You prioritize ease of use over advanced features
  • You send newsletters without complex automation
  • You need a website and email in one place
  • You have simple e-commerce needs (basic abandoned cart)
  • You want beautiful templates with minimal effort

Startups planning to scale should invest in ActiveCampaign early. The CRM and automation foundation grows with you, avoiding a painful migration later.

Migration Considerations

Moving from Mailchimp to ActiveCampaign

Migration is straightforward but requires some work:

  • Contacts: Export from Mailchimp, import to ActiveCampaign (automatic field mapping)
  • Templates: Need to be rebuilt in ActiveCampaign's format
  • Automations: Rebuild using ActiveCampaign's more powerful editor
  • Time required: 1-3 days depending on complexity

Moving from ActiveCampaign to Mailchimp

Going from ActiveCampaign to Mailchimp means losing features:

  • CRM data: No direct equivalent in Mailchimp
  • Complex automations: Must simplify workflows significantly
  • Advanced tracking: Limited in Mailchimp
  • Segment logic: May need manual reconfiguration

Recommendation: Start with Mailchimp if you're unsure, then graduate to ActiveCampaign when you outgrow it. Migrating up is easier than migrating down.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ActiveCampaign better than Mailchimp?

ActiveCampaign is better for businesses needing advanced automation, built-in CRM, and complex customer journeys. Mailchimp is better for beginners, small businesses, and those wanting a free plan. Choose ActiveCampaign for sophisticated workflows; choose Mailchimp for simplicity and low cost.

Which is cheaper: ActiveCampaign or Mailchimp?

Mailchimp has a free plan for up to 500 contacts, making it cheaper for small lists. ActiveCampaign starts at $29/month but offers more features per dollar. For lists over 2,500 contacts, ActiveCampaign often provides better value due to superior automation features.

Can I migrate from Mailchimp to ActiveCampaign?

Yes, migrating from Mailchimp to ActiveCampaign is straightforward. You can export contacts and import them directly. Templates need to be rebuilt, but automation workflows can often be replicated with more advanced features in ActiveCampaign. Migration typically takes 1-2 days.

Does ActiveCampaign have a free plan like Mailchimp?

No, ActiveCampaign does not have a forever-free plan. They offer a 14-day free trial, but all paid plans require a subscription. Mailchimp's free plan supports up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails per month, making it better for testing or very small businesses.

Which is better for e-commerce: ActiveCampaign or Mailchimp?

For e-commerce, ActiveCampaign generally offers more powerful automation for abandoned carts, customer lifecycle, and product recommendations. Mailchimp's e-commerce features are adequate for simple stores. If you integrate with Shopify and need advanced workflows, choose ActiveCampaign.

Does ActiveCampaign have a CRM?

Yes, ActiveCampaign includes a full CRM with deal management, pipeline tracking, and lead scoring on Plus plans and above. This is included at no extra cost. Mailchimp does not offer a CRM—only basic contact management.

Which has better email templates?

Mailchimp has better pre-designed templates and a more intuitive drag-and-drop builder. ActiveCampaign's templates are functional but less polished. If email design is your priority, Mailchimp wins. If automation power matters more, ActiveCampaign is the choice.
