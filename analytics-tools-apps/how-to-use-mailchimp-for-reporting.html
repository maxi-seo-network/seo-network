Mailchimp for Reporting: Email Analytics, Campaign Reports, and Performance Metrics Guide

📋 Key Takeaways

  • Report Types: Campaign reports, audience insights, click maps, e-commerce reports
  • Key Metrics: Open rate, click rate, bounce rate, unsubscribe rate, revenue
  • Integrations: Google Analytics 4, e-commerce platforms, CRMs
  • Export Options: CSV export, API access, scheduled reports
  • Best For: Email marketers tracking campaign performance and ROI
  • Pricing: Free plan includes basic reports; Premium adds advanced analytics

Mailchimp's reporting capabilities are among the most comprehensive in the email marketing space. Whether you're tracking a single campaign or analyzing audience behavior over time, Mailchimp provides the data to understand what's working and what needs improvement.

This guide covers every aspect of Mailchimp reporting—from basic campaign metrics to advanced e-commerce tracking and custom dashboards.

Understanding Mailchimp's Reporting Dashboard

Mailchimp organizes reports into several categories:

1. Campaign Reports

Individual campaign performance including:

  • Open Rate: Percentage of delivered emails that were opened
  • Click Rate: Percentage of opened emails with clicks
  • Bounce Rate: Percentage of emails that couldn't be delivered
  • Unsubscribe Rate: Percentage who unsubscribed from this campaign
  • Abuse Reports: Complaints marked as spam

2. Audience Reports

Audience-level insights including:

  • Growth Trend: Subscriber additions and losses over time
  • Engagement: Most/least engaged contacts
  • Location: Geographic distribution of subscribers
  • Top Email Clients: Where subscribers read emails (Gmail, Apple Mail, etc.)

3. Click Maps

Visual representation showing exactly where subscribers clicked in your email

4. E-commerce Reports (with Integration)

Revenue and conversion tracking:

  • Total revenue attributed to email
  • Average order value from campaigns
  • Purchase funnel from email to checkout

5. Website Reports

Traffic driven from emails to your website (with Google Analytics integration)

Key Email Metrics Explained

Understanding what each metric means and how to interpret it:

📊 Essential Email Metrics

Open Rate

Formula: (Opens ÷ Delivered Emails) × 100

Good benchmark: 20-25% (varies by industry)

What it tells you: Subject line effectiveness, sender reputation, timing

Click Rate (Click-Through Rate)

Formula: (Clicks ÷ Delivered Emails) × 100

Good benchmark: 2-3% (varies by industry)

What it tells you: Email content relevance, CTA effectiveness

Click-to-Open Rate (CTOR)

Formula: (Clicks ÷ Opens) × 100

Good benchmark: 10-15%

What it tells you: Quality of content for those who opened; best for comparing campaigns

Bounce Rate

Formula: (Bounces ÷ Sent Emails) × 100

Good benchmark: Under 2%

What it tells you: List quality, email address validity

Unsubscribe Rate

Formula: (Unsubscribes ÷ Delivered Emails) × 100

Good benchmark: Under 0.5%

What it tells you: Content relevance, frequency, subscriber expectations

Step-by-Step: Accessing Mailchimp Campaign Reports

Viewing Individual Campaign Reports

  1. Navigate to Campaigns from the left sidebar
  2. Click "Email Campaigns" to see all sent campaigns
  3. Find your campaign and click "View Report" on the right
  4. Explore the report tabs:
    • Overview: Summary metrics
    • Activity: Opens, clicks over time
    • Links: Click performance by link
    • Social: Shares and forwards
    • E-commerce: Revenue (if connected)

Understanding the Campaign Report Tabs

Overview Tab

Shows high-level metrics:

  • Sent, delivered, opened, clicked, bounced, unsubscribed
  • Comparison to your account average
  • Industry benchmark comparison

Activity Tab

Shows engagement timing:

  • When subscribers opened (hours after sending)
  • Geographic distribution of opens
  • Email clients used

Links Tab

Shows which links performed best:

  • Total clicks per link
  • Unique clicks per link
  • Click percentage

Click Map

Visual overlay showing where people clicked on your email design

Using Click Maps to Improve Email Design

Click maps are one of Mailchimp's most valuable reporting features:

How to Access Click Maps

  1. Open a campaign report
  2. Click on "Links" tab
  3. Click "View Click Map" button
  4. See your email with click overlays

What Click Maps Tell You

  • Which CTAs get clicked: Are subscribers clicking primary or secondary buttons?
  • Image vs. text links: Do image links or text links perform better?
  • Link placement: Do links above the fold get more clicks?
  • Unused elements: Links that never get clicked might be unnecessary

Optimization Strategies Based on Click Maps

  1. Move high-performing links higher: If footer links get clicks, move them up
  2. Remove unused links: Simplify emails by removing links no one clicks
  3. Test button styles: If image buttons underperform, try text CTAs
  4. Above-fold CTA: Ensure your primary CTA is visible before scrolling

E-commerce Reporting in Mailchimp

When connected to an e-commerce platform, Mailchimp tracks revenue:

Supported E-commerce Integrations

  • Shopify: Full integration with order tracking
  • WooCommerce: Purchase tracking and revenue attribution
  • BigCommerce: Order and product data sync
  • Magento: Customer and order data
  • Squarespace: Commerce integration
  • Stripe: Payment tracking (via integration)

E-commerce Metrics Available

  • Total Revenue: Sales attributed to campaigns
  • Average Order Value: Average purchase from email clicks
  • Conversion Rate: Percentage of clicks that led to purchases
  • Products Purchased: Which products were bought from emails
  • Customer Lifetime Value: Total spend per subscriber

Setting Up E-commerce Tracking

  1. Install Mailchimp app in your e-commerce platform
  2. Connect your store in Mailchimp's Integrations settings
  3. Enable e-commerce tracking in campaign settings
  4. Add product blocks to your emails for automatic product data
  5. View revenue in campaign reports under the E-commerce tab

Integrating Mailchimp with Google Analytics 4

Connect Mailchimp to Google Analytics 4 for comprehensive tracking:

Setup Steps

  1. Go to Integrations in Mailchimp's left sidebar
  2. Find Google Analytics 4 and click "Connect"
  3. Sign in to Google and authorize Mailchimp
  4. Select your GA4 property from the dropdown
  5. Enable tracking for campaigns

What You Can Track

  • Website traffic: Visitors from email campaigns
  • Conversions: Goal completions from emails
  • E-commerce: Transactions attributed to email
  • User behavior: Pages visited after email click
  • Bounce rate: Do email visitors bounce quickly?

UTM Parameters in Mailchimp

Mailchimp automatically adds UTM parameters:


?utm_source=mailchimp
&utm_medium=email
&utm_campaign=[campaign_name]
&utm_id=[campaign_id]

View this data in GA4 under Acquisitions → Source/Medium.

A/B Testing Reports

Mailchimp's A/B testing shows which version performed best:

What You Can Test

  • Subject lines: Test different subject lines
  • From name: Test sender variations
  • Content: Test different email designs
  • Send time: Test different delivery times

Reading A/B Test Reports

  1. Open the campaign report for an A/B test
  2. View the "Winning Combination" section
  3. See open rates for each variation
  4. See confidence level (statistical significance)
  5. Review which version Mailchimp sent to remaining subscribers

Best Practices for A/B Testing

  • Test one variable at a time: Don't test subject line AND content simultaneously
  • Use sufficient sample size: Mailchimp requires at least 5,000 subscribers for A/B tests
  • Wait for significance: Don't declare a winner too early
  • Document results: Keep a log of A/B test learnings

Comparative Reports

Compare campaigns to understand trends:

How to Create Comparative Reports

  1. Go to Reports → Comparative Reports
  2. Select campaigns to compare (up to 12 campaigns)
  3. Choose metrics: Open rate, click rate, revenue, etc.
  4. View side-by-side comparison

What Comparative Reports Reveal

  • Trends over time: Are metrics improving or declining?
  • Campaign performance: Which campaigns performed best?
  • Seasonal patterns: Do certain months perform better?
  • Content impact: Which topics resonate with subscribers?

Exporting Mailchimp Reports

Exporting Campaign Reports

  1. Open a campaign report
  2. Click "Export" in the top right
  3. Choose export format (CSV)
  4. Download the file

What's Included in Export

  • Subscriber email addresses who opened
  • Subscriber email addresses who clicked
  • Timestamps for opens and clicks
  • Links clicked per subscriber
  • Bounce details (hard vs. soft)

Exporting Audience Data

  1. Go to Audience → All contacts
  2. Click "Export Audience"
  3. Receive CSV with all subscriber data

Mailchimp API for Advanced Reporting

For custom reporting, use the Mailchimp API:


// Example: Fetch campaign report via API

const apiKey = 'your_api_key';
const server = 'us1';
const campaignId = 'campaign_id';

fetch(`https://${server}.api.mailchimp.com/3.0/reports/${campaignId}`, {
  method: 'GET',
  headers: {
    'Authorization': `Basic ${btoa('any:' + apiKey)}`,
    'Content-Type': 'application/json'
  }
})
.then(response => response.json())
.then(data => {
  console.log('Open rate:', data.opens.open_rate);
  console.log('Click rate:', data.clicks.click_rate);
});

API Report Endpoints

  • /reports/{campaign_id}: Campaign summary
  • /reports/{campaign_id}/click-details: Link-level data
  • /reports/{campaign_id}/email-activity: Per-subscriber activity
  • /reports/{campaign_id}/locations: Geographic data

Common Reporting Issues and Solutions

❌ Problem

  • Low open rate: Poor subject lines or sender reputation
  • Low click rate: Weak CTAs or irrelevant content
  • High bounce rate: Old or purchased email lists
  • High unsubscribe rate: Sending too frequently or content mismatch
  • Missing e-commerce data: Integration not connected

✅ Solution

  • A/B test subject lines; verify sender domain
  • Improve CTA design; personalize content
  • Clean your list; remove bounced emails
  • Reduce frequency; segment your audience
  • Reconnect e-commerce platform in Integrations

Best Practices for Mailchimp Reporting

  1. Review reports within 48 hours: Email engagement stabilizes quickly
  2. Compare to industry benchmarks: Know your baseline
  3. Track trends, not just individual campaigns: Look for patterns
  4. Segment your analysis: Different audiences may perform differently
  5. Connect Google Analytics: See what happens after the click
  6. Export data regularly: Keep historical records
  7. Act on insights: Reports are only useful if you change behavior

Final Verdict

Mailchimp's reporting capabilities are comprehensive enough for most email marketers. The combination of campaign reports, click maps, audience insights, and e-commerce tracking provides everything needed to understand email performance and optimize campaigns.

The click map feature is particularly valuable—it shows exactly where subscribers clicked, enabling data-driven design improvements. For e-commerce businesses, the revenue tracking makes ROI calculation straightforward.

However, advanced analytics and unlimited API access require the Premium plan ($350/month), which may be overkill for smaller senders. For most businesses, the Standard plan ($20/month) provides sufficient reporting depth.

Best for: Email marketers who need comprehensive reporting without complexity, and e-commerce businesses tracking email-driven revenue.

Not ideal for: Those needing advanced predictive analytics or enterprise-level data warehousing (consider Klaviyo or enterprise tools instead).

Ready to Get Started?

Start with Mailchimp's free plan (includes basic reporting) at mailchimp.com. Upgrade to Standard ($20/month) for comparative reports and e-commerce tracking.
