How to Use FreshBooks for Reporting: Complete Guide to Financial Insights

Last updated: March 2026 | 12 min read

FreshBooks is more than just invoicing software—it's a powerful reporting tool that gives freelancers and small businesses deep insight into their financial health. This comprehensive guide shows you how to leverage FreshBooks' reporting features to make smarter business decisions.

FreshBooks Reports Overview

FreshBooks provides a comprehensive suite of financial reports designed specifically for service-based businesses, freelancers, and agencies. Unlike enterprise accounting software that requires advanced accounting knowledge, FreshBooks reports are intuitive and actionable.

Available Report Types

  • Profit & Loss Report – See your total income minus expenses to understand profitability
  • Expense Report – Track all business expenses by category, vendor, or time period
  • Invoice Report – Monitor invoice status, payments received, and outstanding balances
  • Sales Tax Summary – Calculate sales tax collected for easy tax filing
  • Accounts Aging – See which clients owe you money and how overdue payments are
  • Time Tracking Report – Analyze billable hours by project, client, or team member
  • Client Revenue Report – Identify your most valuable clients and revenue trends

Accessing Reports

To access reports in FreshBooks:

  1. Log into your FreshBooks account
  2. Click on the Reports tab in the left sidebar
  3. Choose the report type you need
  4. Set your date range and filters
  5. View, print, or export the report

Profit & Loss Reports

The Profit & Loss (P&L) report is the most important financial report in FreshBooks. It shows your revenue minus expenses over a selected time period, giving you a clear picture of whether your business is profitable.

How to Generate a P&L Report

  1. Go to Reports → Profit & Loss
  2. Select your date range (monthly, quarterly, or custom range)
  3. Choose whether to include taxes
  4. Click Run Report

Understanding Your P&L Report

Your P&L report includes several key sections:

  • Revenue – Total income from paid invoices during the period
  • Expenses by Category – Breakdown of costs by expense type (e.g., software, travel, supplies)
  • Gross Profit – Revenue minus direct costs
  • Net Profit – Gross profit minus all expenses

Pro Tips for P&L Analysis

  • Compare P&L reports month-over-month to spot trends
  • Set aside time quarterly to review profitability by client
  • Use P&L data to identify unnecessary expenses
  • Share P&L reports with your accountant before tax season

Expense Tracking Reports

Expense reports help you understand where your money goes and identify opportunities to reduce costs. FreshBooks categorizes expenses automatically and lets you filter by date, category, or vendor.

Generating Expense Reports

  1. Navigate to Reports → Expense Report
  2. Select your date range
  3. Filter by category if needed (e.g., "Software", "Travel")
  4. Review the breakdown by category and vendor

Key Metrics to Track

  • Expense Categories – Which categories cost the most?
  • Vendors – Who are your top vendors by spend?
  • Trends – Are expenses increasing or decreasing over time?
  • Ratio – What percentage of revenue goes to expenses?

Tax Deduction Tracking

Every expense in FreshBooks can be marked as tax-deductible. At tax time, run an Expense Report filtered to only show deductible expenses. This makes tax preparation significantly faster and ensures you don't miss deductions.

Invoice & Revenue Reports

Invoice reports reveal critical insights about your cash flow, client payment behavior, and revenue patterns. Understanding these reports helps you optimize your invoicing strategy.

Invoice Status Report

This report shows the current status of all invoices:

  • Draft – Created but not sent
  • Sent – Delivered to client, awaiting payment
  • Viewed – Client opened the invoice
  • Paid – Payment received
  • Overdue – Past due date without payment

Accounts Aging Report

The Accounts Aging report shows outstanding balances organized by how long invoices have been unpaid:

  1. Go to Reports → Accounts Aging
  2. View clients with outstanding balances
  3. Sort by aging period (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+ days)
  4. Send reminders directly from the report

Client Revenue Report

Identify your most valuable clients with the Client Revenue report. This shows total revenue per client over time, helping you:

  • Focus on high-value client relationships
  • Identify clients with declining revenue
  • Plan capacity for future projects
  • Make informed decisions about client acquisition

Time Tracking Reports

If you bill hourly or need to track team productivity, FreshBooks time tracking reports provide detailed insights into how time is spent across projects and clients.

Accessing Time Reports

  1. Go to Reports → Time Entries
  2. Select date range and filters
  3. View by project, client, or team member
  4. Compare billable vs. non-billable hours

Analyzing Time Data

Time reports help you answer questions like:

  • Which projects take the most time?
  • Are your hourly rates covering your actual time investment?
  • Which clients require more time than estimated?
  • How efficiently is your team working?

Billable vs Non-Billable Hours

Understanding the ratio of billable to non-billable hours is crucial for profitability. Aim for at least 70-80% billable hours. If your ratio is lower, consider:

  • Outsourcing administrative tasks
  • Automating repetitive processes
  • Increasing project scope or rates

Tax Reports

FreshBooks simplifies tax preparation by tracking sales tax and organizing expense deductions throughout the year.

Sales Tax Summary

If you collect sales tax, the Sales Tax Summary report shows:

  • Total sales tax collected by jurisdiction
  • Tax collected vs. tax owed
  • Breakdown by tax rate

This is essential for quarterly and annual tax filings.

Year-End Tax Preparation

Before tax season, run these reports:

  1. Annual P&L – Shows total income and expenses for Schedule C
  2. Expense Report – Detailed breakdown of deductible expenses
  3. Sales Tax Summary – For sales tax filings
  4. Time Report – If tracking employee hours for payroll

Export these as PDFs and share with your accountant.

Creating Custom Reports

While FreshBooks offers standard reports, you can create custom views by using filters and exports strategically.

Using Filters Effectively

Most reports allow filtering by:

  • Date range – Custom periods beyond standard options
  • Client – Focus on specific clients
  • Category – Filter expenses by type
  • Status – Invoice status (paid, overdue, etc.)

Exporting for Custom Analysis

For advanced analysis, export reports to CSV and use Excel or Google Sheets:

  1. Run your base report in FreshBooks
  2. Click Export → CSV
  3. Open in your spreadsheet tool
  4. Create custom pivot tables and charts
  5. Combine data from multiple reports

Common Custom Reports

  • Client profitability analysis (combine revenue and time data)
  • Monthly expense trends (pivot expense data)
  • Cash flow forecasting (project based on invoice aging)
  • Team utilization rates (analyze time tracking data)

Best Practices for FreshBooks Reporting

Regular Review Schedule

Establish a consistent reporting cadence:

  • Weekly – Check Accounts Aging for overdue invoices
  • Monthly – Review P&L and Expense reports
  • Quarterly – Deep dive into client profitability and tax preparation
  • Annually – Year-end reports for tax filing

Data Entry Best Practices

Your reports are only as good as your data. Follow these practices:

  • Categorize expenses consistently
  • Log time entries daily (not weekly)
  • Send invoices promptly upon completion
  • Reconcile bank accounts monthly
  • Add notes to unusual transactions

Team Training

If you have a team, ensure everyone understands:

  • How to categorize expenses correctly
  • Time tracking requirements
  • Invoice approval workflows
  • What reports are available and when to use them

Exporting and Sharing Reports

Export Formats

FreshBooks supports multiple export formats:

  • PDF – Best for sharing with clients or accountants
  • CSV – Best for further analysis in spreadsheets
  • Print – Direct printing for physical records

Sharing with Your Accountant

At tax time or for periodic reviews:

  1. Run the annual P&L and Expense reports
  2. Export as PDF
  3. Include a summary email with key observations
  4. Share your FreshBooks login if your accountant needs direct access

Automating Reports

FreshBooks doesn't have native scheduled reports, but you can:

  • Use Zapier to send reports automatically
  • Set calendar reminders to run reports monthly
  • Use the FreshBooks API for custom automation

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I create custom report templates in FreshBooks?

FreshBooks doesn't have custom report templates built-in, but you can save filter settings by bookmarking URLs. For true customization, export to CSV and create spreadsheet templates.

How far back can I view reports?

You can view reports for any period where you have data in FreshBooks. There's no limit to how far back you can go. If you migrated from another system, imported historical data will appear in reports.

Can multiple team members access reports?

Yes, team member permissions can be configured to allow or restrict report access. Admins can see all reports, while employees can be limited to specific areas like time tracking or expenses.

Do FreshBooks reports update in real-time?

Yes, reports reflect current data whenever you run them. There's no delay—paid invoices, new expenses, and time entries appear immediately.

Can I compare periods in reports?

The P&L report allows period comparisons. For example, you can view this month vs. last month, or this year vs. last year. This trend analysis is valuable for spotting business patterns.

Conclusion

FreshBooks reporting transforms raw financial data into actionable insights. By mastering P&L reports, expense tracking, invoice analysis, and time tracking reports, you can:

  • Make data-driven business decisions
  • Identify your most profitable clients and projects
  • Spot expense trends before they become problems
  • Streamline tax preparation with organized records
  • Improve cash flow through better invoice management

Start with weekly Accounts Aging reviews and monthly P&L analysis. As you become comfortable with the basics, explore custom exports and deeper client profitability analysis.
