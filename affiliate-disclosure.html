What Is Affiliate Marketing?

Affiliate marketing is a business model where publishers (like us) earn commissions by promoting products or services from other companies. Here's how it works in simple terms:

  1. We create content about software products (reviews, comparisons, guides)
  2. We include links to those products using special tracking codes
  3. When you click a link and make a purchase, the vendor tracks the sale back to us
  4. The vendor pays us a commission (typically 5-50% of the sale price)
  5. You pay the same price—the commission comes from the vendor's profit margin

This model allows us to provide free, high-quality content while earning revenue from companies rather than charging readers directly.

How Affiliate Links Work

When you click an affiliate link on our website, the process looks like this:

Step What Happens What You See
1. Click You click a link with our affiliate tracking code Normal click—nothing different
2. Track Vendor records that you came from our site Cookie set on your browser
3. Shop You browse and shop normally on the vendor's site Same shopping experience
4. Purchase If you buy within the cookie window, we earn commission Same checkout process, same price
5. Complete Vendor pays us commission from their margin You pay nothing extra

Cookie Windows Explained

Affiliate programs use "cookie windows" to track purchases:

  • Amazon Associates: 24-hour cookie (purchase must be made within 24 hours)
  • Most SaaS Programs: 30-90 day cookies
  • Some Programs: First-click attribution (we get credit if you clicked our link first)

If you click our link but purchase later, we may not earn commission depending on the cookie window. This is why we encourage purchases through our links if you found our content helpful.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does affiliate revenue influence your reviews?

No. Our editorial team operates independently from our commercial partnerships. We recommend products based on genuine research, testing, and analysis—not because we're paid to promote them. If a product has issues or limitations, we report them honestly, even if we have an affiliate relationship with that vendor.

Do I pay more when using affiliate links?

No. The price you pay is exactly the same whether you use our affiliate links or go directly to the vendor's website. The commission comes from the vendor's profit margin, not from your wallet. You pay nothing extra.

How can I support Tools Reviews Hub?

If you find our content helpful and plan to purchase a product we've reviewed, using our affiliate links is a great way to support us at no extra cost to you. Simply click our link before making your purchase. This helps us continue creating free, high-quality content.

What if I don't want to use affiliate links?

That's completely fine! You can navigate directly to any vendor's website without using our links. You'll pay the same price and get the same product. We respect your choice either way.

Do you review products without affiliate links?

Yes. We review products based on merit and reader interest, not just affiliate availability. Some products we review don't have affiliate programs, and we still cover them because they're relevant to our readers.

How do you choose which products to recommend?

We choose products based on quality, features, value, and relevance to our readers. Commission rates do NOT influence our recommendations. We often recommend products with lower commissions (or no affiliate program) over higher-paying alternatives because they're genuinely better for our readers.

Can vendors pay for better reviews?

No. We do NOT accept payment for positive reviews, period. Vendors can provide products for testing, but they cannot buy good ratings. If we review a product negatively, vendors cannot "fix" it with payment. Our reviews are honest and independent.
