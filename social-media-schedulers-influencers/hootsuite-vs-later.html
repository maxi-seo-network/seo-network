Hootsuite vs Later: Best Social Media Scheduler for Influencers (2026 Comparison)

Complete side-by-side comparison of two leading schedulers. Find the right one for your influencer business.

⚡ Quick Verdict

  • Choose Later if: You're an Instagram or TikTok-focused influencer who wants visual planning at an affordable price ($25-80/month)
  • Choose Hootsuite if: You manage 5+ platforms, need team collaboration, or require comprehensive cross-platform analytics ($99-739/month)
  • Price Winner: Later is 4x cheaper for individual influencers ($25/mo vs $99/mo)
  • Instagram Winner: Later offers better visual planning, grid preview, and link-in-bio features
  • Multi-Platform Winner: Hootsuite supports more platforms and offers deeper analytics

Introduction: Two Different Approaches to Scheduling

Hootsuite and Later take fundamentally different approaches to social media scheduling. Hootsuite, founded in 2008, is the veteran—built for enterprise teams managing dozens of accounts across every platform. Later, founded in 2014, is the visual-first specialist, built for Instagram creators before expanding to other platforms.

For influencers, this distinction matters. If you're primarily an Instagram or TikTok creator, Later's visual planning tools and affordable pricing make it compelling. If you're managing a personal brand across LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest simultaneously, Hootsuite's comprehensive platform management might justify the higher cost.

In this detailed comparison, we've tested both platforms extensively for influencer workflows. We'll break down pricing, features, and real-world usability to help you choose.

What's a "Social Set" vs "Account"?

Later counts "social sets"—one social set = one Instagram account, one TikTok account, one Pinterest account, etc. across all platforms. So 1 social set lets you schedule to Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn (6 profiles). Hootsuite counts each profile separately. For an influencer managing 6 profiles, Hootsuite's entry plan works fine. For someone managing 20+ profiles (multiple brands), Hootsuite's mid-tier becomes necessary.

Hidden Costs to Watch

  • Hootsuite: Add-on apps in Hootsuite App Directory can cost $5-50/month extra
  • Later: Linkin.bio is free, but advanced analytics requires Growth tier ($45/mo)

Bottom Line: For individual influencers, Later is dramatically cheaper. For agencies managing 20+ profiles across multiple clients, Hootsuite's per-account pricing may work out better.

📸 Instagram Capabilities: Later Wins for Visual Creators

For Instagram-focused influencers, Later offers a significantly better experience. Here's why:

Grid Preview

Later's drag-and-drop grid preview lets you see exactly how your Instagram feed will look. You can rearrange posts, plan color themes, and ensure visual cohesion—critical for influencers whose brand relies on aesthetic consistency. Hootsuite has a basic drag-drop feature, but it's nowhere near as polished.

Link in Bio

Later includes Linkin.bio free with all plans. This creates a clickable version of your Instagram feed—each post links to its source (blog post, product page, etc.). Hootsuite offers Ow.ly link shortener, but not a full link-in-bio page. For influencers driving traffic from Instagram, Later's Linkin.bio is a significant value-add.

Stories Planning

Later's Stories visual planner lets you storyboard Instagram Stories with templates, text overlays, and stickers before you post. You see exactly how your Stories will appear. Hootsuite can schedule Stories, but the planning experience is more basic.

Reels and TikTok

Both platforms support Reels and TikTok scheduling. Later's integration is smoother—you can preview videos, add trending audio suggestions, and auto-publish. Hootsuite supports scheduling but relies more on push notifications.

When Hootsuite Wins for Instagram

If you're an influencer who also manages comments and DMs at scale (10K+ followers), Hootsuite's unified inbox becomes valuable. You can respond to Instagram comments alongside Twitter mentions and Facebook messages in one place. Later doesn't offer inbox management.

🌐 Multi-Platform Management: Hootsuite's Strength

If your influencer brand spans 5+ platforms—Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Twitter, LinkedIn, Pinterest, and maybe Reddit or Threads—Hootsuite's comprehensive platform support matters.

Platform Coverage

  • Hootsuite: 35+ platforms including YouTube, Reddit, Tumblr, YouTube Studio, and more
  • Later: 6 platforms: Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn

When to Choose Hootsuite for Multi-Platform

  • You're a LinkedIn creator who also posts to Instagram
  • You have a YouTube channel and need to schedule community posts
  • You manage Reddit AMAs or community engagement
  • You're building presence on emerging platforms Hootsuite supports
  • You need one dashboard for all platforms

When Later Is Sufficient

Most influencers operate primarily on Instagram and TikTok, with occasional posts to Facebook and Twitter. For this profile, Later's 6-platform support is adequate. Pinterest support is also excellent for lifestyle and fashion influencers.

📊 Analytics: Enterprise vs Influencer-Focused

Hootsuite Analytics

Hootsuite offers enterprise-grade analytics with custom report builders, competitor analysis, and exportable reports. For agencies proving ROI to influencer clients, this is essential. You can track:

  • Cross-platform engagement trends
  • Best posting times by platform
  • Content performance over time
  • Competitor benchmarking
  • Team productivity metrics
  • Custom dashboards for clients

Later Analytics

Later provides solid analytics for individual influencers:

  • Follower growth charts
  • Best posting times
  • Top-performing content
  • Engagement rate trends
  • Hashtag performance
  • Link clicks from Linkin.bio

For Influencers Selling Brand Deals

Both platforms let you export analytics. Later's analytics are sufficient for most influencer media kits. However, if brands require detailed custom reports or you're managing multiple influencer accounts, Hootsuite's reporting is more robust.

👥 Team Collaboration: Hootsuite for Agencies

If you're an influencer who works with an assistant, editor, or agency, team features matter.

Hootsuite Team Features

  • Role-based permissions: Limit what team members can see and do
  • Approval workflows: Content requires manager approval before posting
  • Team assignments: Assign posts to specific team members
  • Unified inbox: Team can manage comments and DMs together
  • Activity logs: Track who posted what and when

Later Team Features

Later's team features are limited:

  • You can add team members on paid plans
  • Basic collaboration but no approval workflows
  • No inbox management for team comment responses

Verdict: If you're an individual influencer, Later is fine. If you're an agency managing influencer clients, or an influencer with a team of 3+, Hootsuite's collaboration features justify the cost.

🔄 Migration Guide: Switching Between Platforms

Moving from Hootsuite to Later

  1. Export your content: Hootsuite lets you export scheduled posts as CSV
  2. Download media: Download all images and videos from Hootsuite
  3. Import to Later: Bulk upload media to Later's library
  4. Reschedule: Later doesn't support CSV import, so manually reschedule
  5. Update Linkin.bio: Transfer your link-in-bio links to Later
  6. Allow 1-2 weeks: Plan transition time between platforms

Moving from Later to Hootsuite

  1. Export analytics: Download Later analytics reports for historical data
  2. Save Linkin.bio: Your Linkin.bio page won't transfer—recreate in a separate tool or use Ow.ly
  3. Import media: Hootsuite supports bulk image upload
  4. Set up streams: Configure Hootsuite streams for your workflow
  5. Team setup: Add team members and configure permissions
  6. Allow 2-3 weeks: Hootsuite has more setup complexity

Pro Tip: Run both platforms simultaneously for 1-2 weeks during migration to catch any missed content.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hootsuite or Later better for Instagram influencers?

Later is better for Instagram-focused influencers. Its visual grid preview, drag-and-drop planning, and Linkin.bio feature are specifically designed for Instagram's visual nature. Hootsuite can manage Instagram, but its interface is less optimized for visual planning. For Instagram-first creators, Later offers a superior experience at 25% of Hootsuite's price.

How much does Hootsuite cost vs Later in 2026?

Later is significantly cheaper for individual influencers. Later's entry plan starts at $25/month for 1 social set (all platforms), while Hootsuite's cheapest paid plan is $99/month for 10 accounts. For most influencers, Later offers better value. However, for agencies managing 20+ accounts, Hootsuite's pricing becomes more competitive at scale.

Can you schedule Instagram Stories on both platforms?

Yes, both platforms support Instagram Stories scheduling. Later offers a superior visual Stories planner with templates and storyboarding—you can see exactly how your Stories will appear. Hootsuite supports Stories scheduling but with less visual planning capability. Both can auto-post Stories without push notifications.

Which is better for TikTok scheduling: Hootsuite or Later?

Later has better TikTok scheduling capabilities. Both platforms support TikTok, but Later's integration is more polished with auto-publishing, video preview, and TikTok-specific analytics. Later also suggests trending sounds and hashtags. Hootsuite supports TikTok scheduling but relies more on push notifications than true auto-publish.

Does Later have a free plan?

Yes, Later offers a free plan. The free tier includes 1 social set (all platforms), 10 posts per month per platform, and basic analytics. It's genuinely useful for testing the platform or for micro-influencers with low posting volume. Hootsuite only offers a limited-time trial—no permanent free tier.

Can I use both platforms together?

Technically yes, but it's not cost-effective. Some agencies use Later for Instagram/TikTok (visual planning) and Hootsuite for LinkedIn/Twitter (inbox management). However, for individual influencers, paying $124+ per month for both platforms is unnecessary. Choose one that fits your primary platform focus.
