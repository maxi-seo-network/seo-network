System Requirements for Filmora

Filmora runs on most computers, making it accessible for beginners:

Windows Requirements

  • OS: Windows 10/11 (64-bit)
  • CPU: Intel i3 or AMD equivalent
  • RAM: 8GB minimum (16GB recommended)
  • Storage: 10GB free space (plus project files)
  • Graphics: Integrated graphics fine for 1080p

Mac Requirements

  • OS: macOS 10.14 or later
  • CPU: Intel i5 or Apple Silicon M1+
  • RAM: 8GB minimum (16GB for 4K)
  • Storage: 10GB free space

Note: Filmora runs on older hardware better than DaVinci Resolve. If your computer struggles with professional editors, Filmora is a good alternative.

Best Export Settings for YouTube

Filmora includes optimized YouTube presets:

Recommended YouTube Export

  • Preset: YouTube 1080p or YouTube 4K

  • Codec: H.264 (1080p) or H.265 (4K)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Filmora good for beginners on YouTube?

Yes, Filmora is one of the best video editors for YouTube beginners. The drag-and-drop interface, built-in effects library, and simplified timeline make it easy to create polished videos within hours of starting. No prior editing experience required. Most new creators can publish their first video within a day.

How much does Filmora cost in 2026?

Filmora offers several plans: Monthly subscription at $19.99/month, Annual plan at $49.99/year (works out to $4.17/month), and a Perpetual license at $79.99 for the current version only. The AI-powered Filmora Pro costs $49.99/month or $199 lifetime. A free trial is available with watermarks on exports.

Does Filmora have a free version?

Filmora offers a free trial with no time limit. You can use all features including effects, transitions, and AI tools. The only restriction is a watermark on exported videos. To remove watermarks, you need a paid subscription. This lets you fully test the software before committing.

Is Filmora better than DaVinci Resolve?

Filmora is better for beginners who want quick results with built-in effects. DaVinci Resolve is better for creators who want professional color grading and completely free software. Filmora is easier to learn (hours vs weeks), has more built-in assets, but costs money and lacks advanced features. DaVinci Resolve is free, professional-grade, but requires more learning.

Can Filmora edit 4K video?

Yes, Filmora supports 4K video editing on both Windows and Mac. You need a computer with decent specs (8GB+ RAM, modern processor) for smooth 4K playback. The software handles 4K exports without additional cost or upgrades.

Is Filmora worth the subscription?

Filmora is worth the subscription if you value time over money. The built-in stock library alone saves $20-30/month compared to separate stock subscriptions. The AI features (auto-captions, smart cut) save hours of editing time. However, if you're on a tight budget or plan to edit for years, DaVinci Resolve's free version offers better long-term value.

Final Verdict: Is Filmora Worth It for YouTubers?

Filmora is the best choice for beginners who want to create professional-looking YouTube videos without spending weeks learning complex software. The built-in effects library and AI features justify the subscription for creators who value convenience.
