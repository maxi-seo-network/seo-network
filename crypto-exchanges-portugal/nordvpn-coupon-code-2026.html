Security Risks Facing Crypto Traders

Cryptocurrency traders face unique security challenges that make VPN protection essential:

Public WiFi Vulnerabilities: Trading on the go—whether at cafés in Lisbon, airports across Europe, or co-working spaces—exposes you to man-in-the-middle attacks. Hackers can intercept unencrypted traffic, potentially capturing login credentials for exchanges, wallet seed phrases, or private keys.

ISP Tracking and Data Retention: European ISPs may log your browsing activity under data retention laws. While Portugal has relatively privacy-friendly regulations, your trading patterns could still be visible to your internet service provider.

Geo-Restricted Exchange Access: Some cryptocurrency exchanges restrict access based on geographic location. A VPN allows you to securely connect through servers in supported jurisdictions while maintaining privacy.

Targeted Phishing Attacks: Cybercriminals often target crypto holders with sophisticated phishing schemes. VPN usage, combined with threat protection features, can block malicious websites before they load.
