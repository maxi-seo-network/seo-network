Flexible vs Locked Staking on Bybit: Which Is Right for You?

Bybit offers two primary staking structures, each with distinct advantages depending on your investment timeline and liquidity needs.

Flexible Staking

Flexible staking allows withdrawal of staked assets at any time without penalties. Rewards begin accruing immediately and yields are typically credited daily.

  • Withdraw anytime: No lock-up period; instant access
  • Lower APY: Typically 1-5% for major assets
  • Daily yields: Rewards calculated and credited daily
  • No penalties: Unstake whenever needed

Best for: Traders needing quick access, short-term holders, first-time stakers.

Fixed-Term Staking

Fixed-term staking locks assets for a predetermined period (7, 14, 30, 60, or 90 days). In exchange for reduced liquidity, you receive higher APY rates.

  • Locked period: Cannot unstake until term expires
  • Higher APY: Typically 3-15%+ depending on asset and duration
  • APR Booster: Optional boosters can increase cumulative APR by 10%+
  • Settlement at maturity: Rewards typically credited at term end

Best for: Long-term holders, investors confident in positions, those seeking maximum passive income.

Risks and Considerations for Bybit Staking

Platform and Custodial Risk

  • Exchange hacks: No exchange is immune to security breaches despite cold storage and multi-signature protection
  • Regulatory actions: Exchanges can face regulatory shutdowns in certain jurisdictions
  • Insolvency risk: Though Bybit maintains proof-of-reserves, exchange failures have occurred historically

Market Volatility Risk

Staked cryptocurrency remains exposed to price movements. If you stake 1 ETH at $2,400 and earn 5% APY, you'll have 1.05 ETH after a year—but if ETH drops to $1,500, your dollar value decreases despite earning more tokens.

Smart Contract Risk (DeFi Products)

  • Code vulnerabilities: Smart contracts can have bugs that hackers exploit
  • Protocol failures: Underlying DeFi protocols could fail
  • Oracle manipulation: Some products rely on price oracles that can be manipulated

Impermanent Loss (Liquidity Mining)

When providing pairs to AMM pools, price ratio changes can result in your position being worth less than simply holding the assets. Impermanent loss becomes real upon withdrawal.

Liquidity Risk

Fixed-term staking locks assets—you cannot unstake early. You miss the ability to sell during favorable price movements or access funds for emergencies.

Regulatory and Tax Risk

  • Tax obligations: Staking rewards may be taxable events—consult a tax professional
  • Regulatory changes: Governments may restrict staking products
  • Jurisdiction restrictions: Bybit doesn't support US and UK users
