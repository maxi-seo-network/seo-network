Fee Comparison: Binance vs Coinbase for Portuguese Users

Fees are often the deciding factor for cost-conscious Portuguese traders. The difference between these exchanges can amount to hundreds or thousands of euros per year, depending on your trading volume.

Trading Fees Explained

Binance uses a maker-taker fee model starting at 0.1% for spot trading. This can be reduced to 0.075% when you pay fees with Binance Coin (BNB), effectively giving you a 25% discount. High-volume traders can unlock even lower rates through VIP tiers.

Coinbase has a more complex fee structure. The standard Coinbase platform charges between 0.5% to 2% plus a spread (typically around 0.5%). For smaller transactions under €200, they use a flat fee model ranging from €0.99 to €2.99. Coinbase Advanced Trade offers maker-taker fees starting at 0.6%.

Payment Methods for Portuguese Users

Neither exchange directly supports Multibanco, Portugal's popular payment system. However, both offer alternatives:

Bank Transfers (SEPA)

Both Binance and Coinbase support SEPA transfers for EUR deposits and withdrawals. SEPA is the most cost-effective method:

  • Binance: Free SEPA deposits, withdrawals typically €1
  • Coinbase: Free SEPA deposits, free or €0.15 withdrawals

Credit/Debit Cards

Card purchases are instant but more expensive:

  • Binance: 1.8% fee for card purchases
  • Coinbase: 3.99% fee for card purchases (plus spread)

Cryptocurrency Deposits

Both exchanges offer free cryptocurrency deposits. If you already hold crypto elsewhere, you can transfer it to Binance or Coinbase without fees (though network gas fees apply).

Security and Trust

Security is paramount when choosing a cryptocurrency exchange. Both platforms have implemented robust measures, but their track records differ.

Binance Security

  • Two-factor authentication (2FA): Supports Google Authenticator, SMS, and hardware keys
  • Address whitelisting: Restrict withdrawals to approved addresses only
  • Device management: Monitor and control authorized devices
  • SAFU fund: Secure Asset Fund for Users - emergency insurance fund
  • Track record: One hack in 2019 (7,000 BTC stolen, all users fully compensated)

Coinbase Security

  • SOC 2 Type II certified: Enterprise-grade security compliance
  • Two-factor authentication: Supports various 2FA methods
  • Vault accounts: Time-delayed withdrawals for added security
  • Insurance: FDIC insurance on USD balances; crypto insurance for stored assets
  • Track record: No major security breaches to date

Security Winner: Coinbase (by a slight margin)

Coinbase has a cleaner security record with no major hacks. As a US-based, publicly traded company, it adheres to strict regulatory standards including SOC 2 certification. However, Binance's SAFU fund provides excellent protection, and their security has been significantly improved since 2019.

Recommendation: For maximum security, consider transferring large holdings to a hardware wallet like Ledger or Trezor. Neither exchange is a bank — your crypto is safest in your own custody.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Binance available in Portugal?

Yes, Binance is available in Portugal. Portuguese users can register, verify their identity, and access the full range of trading features. Binance supports SEPA transfers for EUR deposits and withdrawals, making it convenient for Portuguese traders.

Is Coinbase available in Portugal?

Yes, Coinbase is available in Portugal and supports SEPA transfers for EUR deposits. However, Portuguese users should note that Coinbase's affiliate program is closed for EU/UK residents, and some features available to US users may not be available in Europe.

Which exchange has lower fees for Portuguese users?

Binance has significantly lower fees. Spot trading starts at 0.1% (reduced to 0.075% with BNB), while Coinbase charges between 0.5% to 2% plus spread. For a €1,000 trade, Binance would charge approximately €1, while Coinbase could charge €5-€20+.

Can I use Multibanco with Binance or Coinbase?

Neither Binance nor Coinbase directly supports Multibanco. Portuguese users typically use SEPA bank transfers (free on both platforms) or credit/debit cards (Binance 1.8%, Coinbase 3.99%). Some users also purchase crypto through local Portuguese exchanges and transfer to Binance or Coinbase.

Is cryptocurrency legal in Portugal?

Yes, cryptocurrency is legal in Portugal. The Portuguese government has taken a relatively crypto-friendly approach. As of 2026, cryptocurrency gains from trading may be subject to taxation under specific circumstances. Always verify current regulations with official Portuguese tax authorities as laws can change.

Which exchange is safer for storing crypto?

Both exchanges have strong security, but for long-term storage, we recommend hardware wallets like Ledger or Trezor. Can I stake crypto on these exchanges in Portugal?
