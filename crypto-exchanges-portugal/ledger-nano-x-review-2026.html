Security Architecture

Secure Element Chip (CC EAL5+)

At the core of Ledger Nano X security is a Secure Element chip certified to CC EAL5+ (Common Criteria Evaluation Assurance Level 5). This is the same grade of security used in credit cards, passports, and military-grade applications. The Secure Element:

  • Isolates private keys from the main processor and memory
  • Prevents extraction of keys even with physical device access
  • Resists side-channel attacks including power analysis and electromagnetic probing
  • Self-destructs after repeated failed PIN attempts (brute force protection)

Critically, your private keys are generated on the device and never leave it. They are never exposed to your computer or phone, even during transactions.

Recovery Phrase (24 Words)

During setup, the Ledger Nano X generates a 24-word recovery phrase using BIP-39 standards. This phrase is the master key to all your cryptocurrencies across all supported networks. Important considerations:

  • Write it down on the provided recovery sheet—never digitally or as a photo
  • Store it offline in a secure location (safe, safety deposit box)
  • Anyone with the phrase can access all your funds—protect it accordingly
  • Multiple copies stored in different locations protects against loss

PIN and Passphrase Protection

The device requires a PIN code (4-8 digits) for access. After 3 incorrect attempts, the device wipes itself, preventing brute force attacks. For additional security, you can enable a passphrase (sometimes called a "25th word"):

  • Passphrase: An additional password that creates a hidden wallet within your device
  • Duress protection: Even if forced to unlock your device, the passphrase-protected wallet remains hidden
  • Plausible deniability: Your main wallet can have minimal funds, while your real holdings are in the passphrase-protected account

Firmware and Software Security

Ledger uses a dual-chip architecture with a Secure Element and a microcontroller. The firmware is partially open-source, with critical cryptographic operations auditable. Ledger has undergone multiple third-party security audits, and their bug bounty program incentivizes responsible disclosure.

Every firmware update is verified by the Secure Element before installation, preventing malicious firmware from compromising the device. Ledger signs all legitimate firmware updates—the device will reject anything unsigned.

Step-by-Step Setup Guide

  1. Purchase from Official Source
    Buy from Ledger's official website only. Never purchase from Amazon, eBay, or unauthorized resellers. Tampered devices have been reported from secondary sources. A genuine device arrives factory-sealed with intact packaging.
  2. Initialize the Device
    Press both buttons simultaneously to turn on. Select "Set up as new device" (or restore from existing recovery phrase if migrating from another wallet).
  3. Set Your PIN Code
    Choose a PIN between 4-8 digits. Use both buttons to select numbers, and right button to confirm. You'll enter it twice. This PIN protects access to your device.
  4. Write Down Your Recovery Phrase
    The device displays 24 words one at a time. Write each word in order on the provided recovery sheet. Double-check each word before proceeding. Never store this phrase digitally or photograph it.
  5. Verify Recovery Phrase
    The device quizzes you on random words from your phrase to confirm you wrote it correctly.
  6. Install Ledger Live
    Download Ledger Live from ledger.com/ledger-live (avoid fake apps on app stores). On mobile, use the official Ledger Live app from iOS App Store or Google Play.
  7. Connect and Verify
    Connect via USB or Bluetooth. Ledger Live guides you through device verification. Allow Ledger Manager on your device to manage apps.
  8. Install Coin Apps
    In Ledger Live, navigate to Manager and install apps for cryptocurrencies you hold. Install the Bitcoin app for Bitcoin, Ethereum app for ETH and ERC-20 tokens, etc.
  9. Add Accounts
    In the Accounts tab, add accounts for each cryptocurrency. You can create multiple accounts per coin for organization.
  10. Transfer Your Assets
    Use the Receive function in Ledger Live to get addresses for each cryptocurrency. Send small test amounts first to verify everything works before transferring larger holdings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ledger Nano X safe for storing cryptocurrency?

Yes, Ledger Nano X has never had a successful hack of user funds. The CC EAL5+ certified Secure Element keeps private keys isolated and resistant to physical attacks. However, security also depends on user practices: never share your recovery phrase, buy only from official sources, and verify addresses on the device screen before confirming transactions.

What happens if I lose my Ledger Nano X?

Your cryptocurrency remains safe on the blockchain. Use your 24-word recovery phrase to restore your wallet on any new Ledger device or compatible wallet (like Electrum for Bitcoin, MetaMask for Ethereum). This is why writing down and safely storing your recovery phrase during setup is absolutely critical—without it, your funds are permanently inaccessible.

Can Ledger Nano X hold all cryptocurrencies?

Ledger Nano X supports over 5,500 cryptocurrencies including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and all major chains. However, some newer or less popular coins may not be supported. Check Ledger's official compatibility list if you hold specific assets. The device can store multiple cryptocurrencies simultaneously.

Can I use Ledger Nano X with my iPhone?

Yes, Ledger Nano X works with iPhone via Bluetooth and is currently the only hardware wallet that supports iOS. Download the Ledger Live app from the App Store, enable Bluetooth on your Ledger Nano X, and follow the pairing instructions. You can check balances, send transactions, and manage your portfolio directly from your iPhone.

How long does the Ledger Nano X battery last?

The Ledger Nano X battery lasts approximately 8 hours of active use and several weeks in standby mode. The battery charges via USB-C in about 90 minutes. Note that the battery is not user-replaceable—if it degrades after several years, the device will still function when connected via USB. Ledger estimates battery life of 5+ years with normal use.

Where should I buy Ledger Nano X?

Only buy from Ledger's official website (shop.ledger.com). Never purchase from Amazon, eBay, Craigslist, or unauthorized resellers. There have been documented cases of tampered devices sold through secondary markets where attackers pre-configured recovery phrases to steal funds. A genuine device from Ledger arrives factory-sealed with intact packaging and generates a fresh recovery phrase on first use.

Is Ledger Nano X better than Trezor?

Neither is universally "better"—they serve different needs. Ledger Nano X offers Bluetooth, mobile support, iOS compatibility, and 5,500+ coins. Trezor offers 100% open-source firmware and Shamir backup (splitting recovery phrase into multiple shares). Choose Ledger for mobile/portable use and maximum coin support. Choose Trezor for open-source transparency and advanced recovery options.
