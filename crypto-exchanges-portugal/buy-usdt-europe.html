What is USDT (Tether)?

USDT (Tether) is a stablecoin - a cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value of $1 USD. Key facts:

  • Price: Pegged 1:1 to USD (approximately €0.92)
  • Market cap: Over $100 billion (largest stablecoin)
  • Use cases: Trading, hedging, transfers, DeFi
  • Blockchains: Ethereum, Tron, BSC, Solana, and more

Why Buy USDT?

1. Stability

USDT maintains a stable $1 value, protecting you from crypto volatility without converting to fiat.

2. Trading

Most trading pairs are USDT pairs. Having USDT allows you to quickly enter and exit positions.

3. Transfers

Send value globally with low fees, especially on Tron (TRC-20) or BSC (BEP-20) networks.

4. DeFi

Use USDT in lending protocols, liquidity pools, and yield farming.

USDT Networks Explained

USDT exists on multiple blockchains. They're all the same USDT, but on different networks:

ERC-20 (Ethereum)

  • Original USDT network
  • Highest fees ($5-50 per transaction)
  • Required for Ethereum DeFi
  • Most widely supported

TRC-20 (Tron)

  • Lowest fees (~$1)
  • Fast transactions
  • Best for transfers between exchanges
  • Most popular for withdrawals

BEP-20 (BSC)

  • Very low fees (~$0.10)
  • Fast transactions
  • Best for Binance ecosystem
Warning: Never send USDT on the wrong network! ERC-20 USDT sent to a TRC-20 address will be lost forever.

Is USDT Safe?

Risks to Consider:

  • Centralization: Tether Limited controls USDT reserves
  • Transparency: Reserves are audited but debates continue
  • Regulatory risk: Governments may regulate stablecoins
  • Depeg risk: Rare but possible (USDT briefly dropped below $1 in 2022)

Alternatives:

  • USDC: More transparent, fully audited
  • EUR stablecoins: EURC, EURT (pegged to Euro)
