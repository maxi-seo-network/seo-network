What is USDT and Why Buy It?

Understanding Tether (USDT)

USDT, also known as Tether, is a stablecoin — a type of cryptocurrency designed to minimize price volatility. Each USDT token is intended to be backed 1:1 by US dollar reserves held by Tether Limited. This means that one USDT should always be worth approximately one US dollar, though small fluctuations can occur.

USDT was launched in 2014 and has since become the most widely traded stablecoin in the cryptocurrency ecosystem. As of 2026, it has a market capitalization exceeding $100 billion and is available on multiple blockchain networks including Ethereum (ERC-20), Tron (TRC-20), BNB Chain (BEP-20), Solana, and several others.

DeFi Participation: The decentralized finance (DeFi) ecosystem runs largely on stablecoins. USDT is accepted across virtually every DeFi protocol, lending platform, and decentralized exchange. Holding USDT opens doors to yield farming, liquidity provision, and other DeFi opportunities.

Remittances and Transfers: USDT can be sent globally in minutes with minimal fees compared to traditional bank transfers. This is especially valuable for Europeans with family abroad who need to send value quickly and affordably.

Trading Pair Flexibility: On most exchanges, USDT serves as the base currency for trading pairs. If you want to buy altcoins, you'll often need to first convert your EUR or crypto into USDT. Having USDT readily available gives you instant access to thousands of trading opportunities.

Step 2: Complete Identity Verification (KYC)

European regulations require all legitimate exchanges to verify user identity before allowing EUR deposits. This process is called Know Your Customer (KYC) verification.

Prepare the following documents:

  • Government-issued photo ID (passport, national ID card, or driver's license)
  • Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement, or tax document from the last 3 months)

The verification process typically takes 10 minutes to 48 hours depending on the exchange and verification volume. Most users are verified within a few hours.

Step 3: Deposit EUR into Your Account

Once verified, you can deposit euros. Navigate to the "Deposit" or "Fiat and Spot" section of your exchange.

Option A: SEPA Bank Transfer (Recommended)

SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area) transfers are the most cost-effective way to deposit EUR. Most exchanges offer free SEPA deposits, though your bank may charge a small fee (typically €0-2). SEPA transfers usually complete within 1-2 business days.

To make a SEPA deposit:

  1. Select EUR as the deposit currency
  2. Choose SEPA bank transfer
  3. Copy the exchange's bank details and your unique reference code
  4. Initiate the transfer from your bank account
  5. Wait for the funds to arrive (1-2 business days)

Option B: Credit/Debit Card

Card deposits are instant but come with higher fees. Expect to pay 1.5-3.5% on card deposits. This option is best for those who need USDT immediately and don't mind paying a premium for speed.

Step 4: Navigate to the USDT Trading Pair

After your EUR deposit arrives, go to the trading section of the exchange. Search for "USDT" and select the EUR/USDT trading pair. This allows you to buy USDT directly with euros.

Alternatively, some exchanges offer a "Convert" or "Buy Crypto" feature that simplifies the process with a single-click purchase interface.

Step 5: Place Your Buy Order

You'll see several order types. For beginners, a "Market" order is the simplest:

Market Order: Buys USDT immediately at the current market price. Enter the amount of EUR you want to spend or the amount of USDT you want to buy, then confirm the purchase. Your order executes instantly.

Limit Order: Sets a specific price at which you want to buy USDT. If the market price reaches your limit, your order executes. This is useful if you believe the price might drop slightly and want to wait for a better rate.

Once your order fills, you'll see USDT in your exchange wallet balance.

Step 6: Secure Your USDT

For small amounts, keeping USDT on a reputable exchange like Binance is generally safe. However, for larger holdings, consider transferring to a hardware wallet like Ledger or Trezor. These devices store your private keys offline, providing protection against hacks and exchange failures.

Security Tips for USDT Buyers

Your USDT investment is only as safe as your security practices. Follow these essential tips to protect your assets:

Use Strong Authentication

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your exchange account immediately. Use an authenticator app like Google Authenticator or Authy rather than SMS-based 2FA, which is vulnerable to SIM-swapping attacks. This adds a critical layer of protection even if your password is compromised.

Whitelist Withdrawal Addresses

Most exchanges offer address whitelisting, which restricts withdrawals to pre-approved wallet addresses only. Enable this feature and add your hardware wallet address. Even if hackers access your account, they cannot withdraw to unknown addresses.

Consider a Hardware Wallet

For holdings exceeding €1,000, strongly consider a hardware wallet like Ledger or Trezor. These devices store your private keys offline, making them immune to online hacks. Hardware wallets cost €60-200 but provide peace of mind that's worth far more.

Beware of Phishing Scams

Scammers create fake exchange websites and send fraudulent emails to steal login credentials. Always verify the URL carefully (look for the correct domain and HTTPS). Never click links in unexpected emails. Bookmark your exchange's official website and access it directly.

Enable Anti-Phishing Codes

Binance and other exchanges let you set an anti-phishing code that appears in all legitimate emails from the platform. If an email claims to be from your exchange but lacks this code, it's a scam.

Understand USDT Network Types

USDT exists on multiple blockchains. When withdrawing or receiving USDT, ensure you select the correct network:

  • ERC-20: Ethereum network. Most expensive fees, highest security, widely supported.
  • TRC-20: Tron network. Very low fees, widely supported.
  • BEP-20: BNB Chain. Low fees, excellent for Binance ecosystem.

Sending USDT to an incompatible network address may result in permanent loss of funds. Always double-check the network before confirming any transfer.

Keep Records for Taxes

Cryptocurrency transactions may be taxable events in your EU country. Keep detailed records of your purchases, including dates, amounts, prices, and transaction IDs. Many exchanges provide downloadable transaction history for tax purposes. Consult a local tax professional for country-specific guidance.
