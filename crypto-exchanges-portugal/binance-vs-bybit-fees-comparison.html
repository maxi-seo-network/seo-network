Spot Trading Fees Comparison

Spot trading fees are what you pay every time you buy or sell cryptocurrency on the exchange's main market. Both Binance and Bybit use a maker-taker fee model:

  • Maker: Fee paid when you add liquidity (limit orders that aren't immediately filled)
  • Taker: Fee paid when you remove liquidity (market orders or limit orders that fill immediately)

How the BNB Discount Works

Binance Coin (BNB) is Binance's native cryptocurrency. When you hold BNB and enable "Pay fees with BNB," you receive a 25% discount on all trading fees. This reduces your fee from 0.1% to 0.075%.

Example: Trading €10,000 worth of Bitcoin
  • Standard fee (0.1%): €10.00
  • With BNB discount (0.075%): €7.50
  • Savings: €2.50 per trade

Over 100 trades, that's €250 in savings. The discount applies automatically when enabled.

Bybit has no equivalent discount token, making Binance slightly cheaper for regular traders.

Hidden Fees & Spreads

Beyond the explicit fees, there are hidden costs to consider:

Spread Markup

The spread is the difference between the buy (ask) and sell (bid) price. Exchanges with "zero fee" promotions often widen spreads to compensate:

  • Binance Spot: Generally tight spreads due to high liquidity
  • Binance Convert: May have wider spreads than spot trading
  • Bybit Spot: Competitive spreads, similar to Binance
  • Bybit Convert: Wider spreads during high volatility

Recommendation: For the best price, use the spot trading interface rather than the "Convert" or "Buy Crypto" buttons. The explicit fees are often lower than the hidden spread markup.

Conversion Fees

If your account holds EUR but you want to buy a crypto that only trades against USDT, you'll pay two spreads:

  1. EUR → USDT conversion (spread)
  2. USDT → Crypto purchase (trading fee)

Tip: If you plan to trade actively, consider converting EUR to USDT once (paying one spread), then trading within USDT pairs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Binance have hidden fees?

Binance is relatively transparent, but be aware of: spread markup in "Convert" features, dynamic withdrawal fees that change with network conditions, and inactivity fees on some Binance products (not regular trading). Always review the total cost preview before confirming any transaction.

Is Bybit cheaper than Binance?

For most users, Bybit is slightly more expensive due to Binance's BNB discount (25% off fees). However, the difference is small (0.025% per trade). For futures trading, the difference is negligible. For very high volume traders, Bybit's 0% maker fee at VIP Pro level could be advantageous.

How do I get 0% fees on Binance?

You cannot get 0% fees on regular spot trading. However, Binance occasionally offers zero-fee promotions for specific pairs (Bitcoin zero-fee events). Binance also has zero-fee trading for certain stablecoin pairs. VIP 4+ users can get maker fees as low as 0.02%.

Which exchange is better for small traders?

For small traders (under €1,000/month), Binance is better due to the BNB discount. The minimum fee savings per trade may be small (cents), but they compound. For very small amounts (under €50), the difference is negligible — choose based on interface preference.

Do I need to buy BNB to save fees?

Yes, to get Binance's 25% fee discount, you need to hold some BNB in your account and enable "Pay fees with BNB." The BNB used for fees is automatically deducted. You can buy as little as €20-50 worth of BNB and it will last for many trades.

Are withdrawal fees negotiable?

Generally no — withdrawal fees are set by the exchange to cover network transaction costs. However, VIP users on both platforms may get reduced withdrawal fees. The best strategy is to minimize the number of withdrawals by consolidating them.

Final Verdict: Binance Wins on Fees

For most traders, Binance offers lower overall fees thanks to the BNB discount and more aggressive VIP tier pricing. The 25% fee reduction (from 0.1% to 0.075%) compounds over time, and Binance's superior liquidity often results in tighter spreads.

However, Bybit is competitive and may be preferable for derivatives-focused traders or those who prefer its cleaner interface. The fee difference for typical retail traders amounts to tens of euros per year — not enough to justify switching if you prefer Bybit's platform.

Our recommendation: Start with Binance for the lowest fees and enable the BNB discount. If you become a heavy derivatives trader, consider Bybit for its specialized futures platform.
