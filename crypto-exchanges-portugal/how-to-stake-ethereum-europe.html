What is Ethereum Staking?

Ethereum staking is the process of locking your ETH to help secure the Ethereum network. In return, you earn rewards paid in ETH. Since Ethereum's transition to Proof-of-Stake (The Merge in 2022), staking has become the primary way to earn passive income on ETH.

Risks of Ethereum Staking

Smart Contract Risk

Staking through exchanges involves smart contract risk. If the exchange's smart contract has a bug, you could lose funds.

Slashing Risk

Validators can be slashed (penalized) for malicious behavior or downtime. Exchanges like Binance absorb this risk for retail stakers.

Liquidity Risk

Staked ETH is locked until the Ethereum withdrawal mechanism is fully enabled. With Binance's liquid staking, you can unstake earlier but may pay a fee.

Price Risk

If ETH price drops, your staked ETH is still worth less. Staking rewards don't protect against price drops.
