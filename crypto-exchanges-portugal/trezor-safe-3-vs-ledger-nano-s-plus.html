Trezor Safe 3 vs Ledger Nano S Plus: Complete Comparison Guide 2026

Choosing your first hardware wallet can feel overwhelming. With so many options on the market, how do you know which one truly protects your cryptocurrency? Why Hardware Wallets Matter for Crypto Security

Before diving into the comparison, let's address why you need a hardware wallet in the first place. When you store cryptocurrency on an exchange like Binance or Coinbase, you don't actually control your private keys. The exchange holds them for you—and history has shown this can end badly.

Exchange hacks, insolvency events, and frozen accounts have cost investors billions. A hardware wallet stores your private keys offline, in a physical device that never exposes them to the internet. This makes it virtually impossible for remote hackers to steal your funds.

Both Trezor and Ledger have built strong reputations in this space. Trezor, a Czech company, launched the world's first hardware wallet in 2014. Ledger, a French company, has become equally prominent with its line of secure devices. Both companies have years of experience and millions of satisfied customers.

Trezor Safe 3: Overview and Key Features

The Trezor Safe 3 represents the evolution of Trezor's approach to accessible security. Released in 2023, it succeeds the popular Trezor Model One while adding significant security improvements.

Security Architecture

The standout feature of the Trezor Safe 3 is its dedicated Secure Element chip. This EAL6+ certified component provides an additional layer of protection against physical attacks. If someone steals your device, they cannot extract your private keys even with physical access to the hardware.

Trezor has always championed open-source software, and the Safe 3 continues this tradition. Every aspect of the firmware is publicly auditable. Security researchers worldwide can review the code, identify vulnerabilities, and verify that no backdoors exist. This transparency appeals to users who prioritize trust minimization.

The device also supports Shamir Backup, allowing you to split your recovery seed into multiple parts. Instead of relying on a single 12-24 word phrase, you can create up to 16 shares that require a subset to recover your wallet. This provides redundancy if part of your backup gets lost or compromised.

User Experience

Trezor Safe 3 features a simple OLED display (128×64 pixels) with two physical buttons for navigation. The interface prioritizes clarity over flashiness. You'll see transaction details on screen before confirming, allowing you to verify what you're signing.

The device connects via USB-C and works with the Trezor Suite desktop application or web interface. Trezor Suite provides a clean dashboard for managing your portfolio, sending transactions, and accessing advanced features like coin control.

Supported Cryptocurrencies

Trezor Safe 3 supports thousands of cryptocurrencies, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, XRP, Bitcoin Cash, Dogecoin, Zcash, Cardano, and countless ERC-20 tokens. The device handles all major blockchain networks and integrates with third-party wallets like MetaMask, Electrum, and BlueWallet for extended functionality.

Ledger Nano S Plus: Overview and Key Features

The Ledger Nano S Plus launched in 2022 as an upgrade to the original Nano S. It addresses the storage limitations of its predecessor while maintaining the compact form factor that made the Nano S popular.

Security Architecture

Ledger takes a different approach to security. The Nano S Plus features a Secure Element chip with CC EAL6+ certification—the highest security certification available for consumer hardware wallets. This chip isolates your private keys and signs transactions in a secure environment.

The device uses Ledger's proprietary operating system, BOLOS (Blockchain Open Ledger Operating System). This allows you to install multiple cryptocurrency apps simultaneously, each isolated from the others. If one app has a vulnerability, it cannot affect the others or expose your keys.

Unlike Trezor's open-source approach, Ledger keeps some aspects of its firmware proprietary. This has sparked debates in the crypto community about transparency versus security through obscurity. Both approaches have valid arguments, and ultimately both companies have maintained strong security records.

User Experience

The Ledger Nano S Plus features a slightly larger LED screen (128×64 pixels) compared to its predecessor. Navigation uses two physical buttons on the device. While the interface works well, some users find the small screen challenging for complex transactions.

Ledger Live, the companion application, offers a polished experience. You can manage your portfolio, buy crypto directly through integrated partners, swap tokens, stake certain cryptocurrencies, and even manage NFTs. The app supports over 5,500 crypto assets across multiple blockchain networks.

The device can store up to 100 different cryptocurrency apps simultaneously—a significant improvement over the original Nano S, which was limited to about 3-5 apps depending on their size.

Supported Cryptocurrencies

Ledger Nano S Plus supports the widest range of cryptocurrencies in its price range. Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, XRP, and thousands of other assets work seamlessly. The Ledger Live app provides native support for most major networks, while third-party integrations extend functionality further.

Key Differences Explained

Open Source Philosophy

This represents the most fundamental difference between the two wallets. Trezor Safe 3 is completely open source. Anyone can review, audit, and verify the firmware. This transparency aligns with Bitcoin's ethos of "don't trust, verify."

Ledger takes a more traditional approach. While they publish some code, the core BOLOS operating system remains proprietary. Some users prefer this model, arguing that proprietary code is harder for attackers to study and exploit. Others prefer complete transparency.

Shamir Backup vs Standard Recovery

Trezor Safe 3 includes Shamir Backup functionality, allowing you to split your recovery seed into multiple shares. For example, you might create five shares, any three of which can recover your wallet. This provides flexibility for inheritance planning or distributed trust.

Ledger Nano S Plus uses the standard BIP39 recovery phrase. While this works reliably, it creates a single point of failure. If you lose your seed phrase, you lose access to your funds permanently. You can manually implement similar protection using third-party tools, but it's not built into the device.

Software Ecosystem

Ledger Live offers more integrated features out of the box. You can buy cryptocurrency directly through the app, swap tokens, stake supported assets, and manage NFTs—all without connecting third-party services. This convenience appeals to users who want an all-in-one solution.

Trezor Suite provides solid core functionality but relies more heavily on third-party integrations for advanced features. For users comfortable with external tools like MetaMask or Electrum, this isn't a limitation. But beginners might appreciate Ledger's unified experience.

Setting Up Your First Hardware Wallet

Regardless of which device you choose, follow these security best practices:

  1. Buy directly from the manufacturer. Never purchase hardware wallets from third-party sellers like eBay or Amazon. Compromised devices have been sold in the past.
  2. Verify the tamper-evident seal. Both Trezor and Ledger use security seals. If they appear damaged or missing, contact customer support immediately.
  3. Write down your recovery seed offline. Never type your seed phrase into a computer or phone. Use the provided recovery sheets and store them securely.
  4. Consider a metal backup solution. Paper can burn, get wet, or degrade over time. Metal seed storage devices provide better long-term protection.
  5. Enable PIN protection. Both devices require a PIN. Choose something memorable but not easily guessed.
  6. Test your recovery. Before storing significant funds, practice recovering your wallet using your seed phrase. This ensures you wrote everything correctly.
