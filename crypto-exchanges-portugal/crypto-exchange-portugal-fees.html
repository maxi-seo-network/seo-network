Understanding Trading Fees

Maker vs Taker Fees

  • Maker: When you place a limit order that adds liquidity to the order book
  • Taker: When you place a market order that removes liquidity from the order book

For most beginners, market orders (taker fees) are more common.

Hidden Fees to Watch For

1. Spread

The difference between buy and sell price. Some exchanges (like Coinbase) have large spreads.

2. Network Fees

When withdrawing crypto, you pay blockchain network fees. These vary by network:

Network Approximate Fee
Ethereum (ERC-20) $5-50 depending on gas
Bitcoin $2-10
BSC (BEP-20) $0.10-0.50

Is it free to deposit EUR on crypto exchanges?

Why are Coinbase fees so high?

Coinbase charges premium fees for ease of use. The spread (hidden in price) + transaction fee can total 1.5-2% or more.

Are network fees included in exchange fees?

No. Exchange fees and network fees are separate. You pay network fees when withdrawing crypto to an external wallet.
