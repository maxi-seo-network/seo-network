Trezor Safe 3 Security Features

EAL6+ Secure Element Chip

The most significant upgrade over previous Trezor models is the inclusion of an EAL6+ certified Secure Element. This chip is the same security certification used in banking smart cards and passports. It provides:

  • Physical attack resistance — Protection against tampering, side-channel attacks, and hardware exploits
  • Secure key storage — Private keys are stored in the secure chip, never exposed to the main processor
  • Pin verification — PIN entry happens directly on the device, not the potentially compromised computer

100% Open-Source Firmware

Unlike competitors like Ledger, Trezor's firmware is completely open-source. Every line of code can be independently audited by security researchers, developers, and users. This transparency ensures:

  • No hidden backdoors or secret data collection
  • Community verification of security claims
  • Independent reproducible builds
  • Trust through verification, not corporate promises
Why Open Source Matters: Closed-source wallets require you to trust the manufacturer's claims. Open-source wallets like Trezor allow independent experts to verify those claims. For storing significant wealth, this transparency is invaluable.

On-Device Confirmation

Every transaction requires physical confirmation on the device. Even if your computer is infected with malware, the attacker cannot authorize transactions without physical access to your Trezor Safe 3 and knowledge of your PIN.

PIN and Passphrase Protection

Trezor Safe 3 supports:

  • PIN protection — Set a PIN up to 50 digits to prevent unauthorized device access
  • Passphrase wallet — Add a secret passphrase (25th word) for an additional hidden wallet layer
  • On-device entry — Both PIN and passphrase are entered using the physical buttons, never typed on your computer

Shamir Backup (Multi-share Backup)

Trezor Safe 3 supports Shamir's Secret Sharing Scheme for backup. Instead of one recovery seed, you can split your backup into multiple shares (e.g., 3-of-5). To recover your wallet, you need a minimum number of shares, but not all of them. This provides:

  • Redundancy — lose some shares but still recover your wallet
  • Security — no single point of failure for your backup
  • Flexibility — store shares in different locations

Setup Guide: Getting Started with Trezor Safe 3

Step 1: Purchase from Official Source

Critical: Only buy from the official Trezor.io store or authorized resellers listed on Trezor's website. Never buy from Amazon, eBay, or second-hand markets. Tampered devices can steal your cryptocurrency.

Step 2: Verify the Holographic Seal

When your Trezor arrives, check that the holographic security seal on the box is intact. If it appears tampered with or missing, contact Trezor support immediately and do not use the device.

Step 3: Initialize the Device

  1. Connect your Trezor Safe 3 to your computer using the included USB-C cable
  2. Visit trezor.io/start in your browser
  3. Download and install Trezor Suite (available for Windows, macOS, Linux)
  4. Follow the on-screen instructions to install firmware
  5. Choose "Create new wallet" when prompted

Step 4: Create Your PIN

Set a strong PIN (up to 50 digits). This protects your device if someone physically steals it. Enter the PIN using the two physical buttons on the device—never type it on your computer.

Step 5: Write Down Your Recovery Seed

The device will display your recovery seed (12, 20, or 24 words). This is your only backup if the device is lost, stolen, or damaged.

Critical Security Rules:
  • Write your seed on paper or metal backup—never digitally
  • Never photograph your seed or store it on any electronic device
  • Never share your seed with anyone—not even Trezor support
  • Store multiple copies in separate secure locations

Step 6: Install Coin Apps

Through Trezor Suite, install apps for the cryptocurrencies you want to manage. Popular options include Bitcoin, Ethereum, Cardano, Solana, and many others.
