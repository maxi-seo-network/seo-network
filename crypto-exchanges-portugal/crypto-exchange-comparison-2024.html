Frequently Asked Questions

Which exchange has the lowest fees in Europe?

Binance and Bybit typically offer the lowest trading fees at 0.1% for spot trading. Binance provides an additional 25% discount when paying fees with BNB token, bringing the effective rate to 0.075%. However, always check current fee schedules as they may change.

What is the safest crypto exchange for Europeans?

Kraken and Bitstamp are considered among the safest exchanges for European users, with strong regulatory compliance, excellent security records, and proper licensing. Kraken has never suffered a major hack, and Bitstamp was the first EU-licensed exchange.

Do I need to verify my identity to use crypto exchanges?

Yes, all regulated exchanges operating in Europe require KYC (Know Your Customer) verification. You'll need to provide a government-issued ID (passport, national ID, or driver's license) and proof of address to create an account and trade.

Can I buy crypto with EUR on these exchanges?

Yes, all five exchanges in our comparison support EUR deposits. Binance, Kraken, Bitstamp, and Bitpanda offer SEPA bank transfers. Bitpanda additionally supports local payment methods like Sofort and EPS. Bybit primarily supports card purchases for EUR funding.

Should I keep my crypto on an exchange or in a wallet?

For small amounts you're actively trading, keeping crypto on a reputable exchange is acceptable. For larger holdings or long-term storage, a hardware wallet like Ledger or Trezor is strongly recommended. Remember: "Not your keys, not your crypto" - exchanges can fail, freeze accounts, or be hacked.

Can I use multiple exchanges?

Absolutely! Many traders use multiple exchanges to access different coins, compare prices for arbitrage, and diversify their counterparty risk. For example, you might use Binance for its coin selection, Kraken for security, and Bybit for derivatives.

What happens if an exchange goes bankrupt?

Exchange bankruptcies have happened (most notably FTX in 2022). While some exchanges have insurance funds (Binance's SAFU), these may not cover all losses. This is why storing significant holdings in your own wallet is crucial - exchanges are for trading, not long-term storage.
