What is Staking?

Staking is the process of locking your cryptocurrency to support a blockchain network and earning rewards in return. It's similar to earning interest in a savings account, but for crypto.

How Staking Works:

  1. You deposit cryptocurrency into a staking pool
  2. Your crypto helps secure the network (proof-of-stake)
  3. You earn rewards in the same cryptocurrency
  4. Rewards are typically paid daily or weekly

Binance Staking Options

Binance offers two types of staking:

1. Locked Staking

  • Higher APY (Annual Percentage Yield)
  • Crypto is locked for a set period (7, 14, 30, 60, 90 days)
  • Cannot withdraw until lock period ends
  • Best for: Long-term holders

2. Flexible Staking

  • Lower APY
  • Can withdraw anytime
  • No lock period
  • Best for: Short-term holders

Risks of Staking

Important Risks to Consider:
  • Price volatility: Your staked crypto can lose value
  • Lock periods: Can't sell during lock period even if price drops
  • Smart contract risk: Bugs in staking contracts
  • Exchange risk: Binance holds your keys
  • Regulatory risk: Staking may face regulatory changes

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Binance staking safe?

Binance staking is relatively safe, but carries risks: exchange risk (Binance holds your keys), smart contract risk, and price volatility risk.

What's the minimum to stake?

Minimum amounts vary by cryptocurrency. Some start at 0.01 coins, others require more.

Can I lose money staking?

Yes. The value of your staked crypto can decrease. You can also lose money if Binance or the blockchain network has issues.

How often are rewards paid?

Most staking rewards are distributed daily to your spot wallet.

Should I stake on Binance or stake myself?

Binance staking is easier but you give up control of your keys. Self-staking gives you full control but requires technical knowledge.
