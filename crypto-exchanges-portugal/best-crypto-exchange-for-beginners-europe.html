  • Responsive Support: Help when you need it, ideally via live chat

Step-by-Step: Creating Your First Account

Ready to start? Here's how to create your account on any European crypto exchange:

  1. Choose Your Exchange
    Based on your priorities, select an exchange. For lowest fees, choose Binance. For easiest experience, choose Coinbase. For security, choose Kraken.
  2. Create Your Account
    Visit the exchange website and click "Sign Up" or "Get Started." Use your personal email address (not work email) and create a strong, unique password. Consider using a password manager.
  3. Verify Your Email
    Check your inbox for a verification email. Click the link to confirm your email address. This is a security essential — if you can't verify your email, you won't be able to use the exchange.
  4. Complete Identity Verification (KYC)
    European regulations require identity verification. Prepare:
    • Passport or national ID card (photos of front and back)
    • Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement from last 3 months)
    • Selfie (sometimes required for facial verification)
    Verification typically takes 5-30 minutes on modern exchanges.
  5. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
    Before depositing any money, enable 2FA using an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, or 1Password). Avoid SMS-based 2FA if possible — it's less secure than app-based codes.
  6. Link Your Payment Method
    For European users, SEPA bank transfers are recommended for lowest fees. Link your bank account by providing your IBAN and following the verification steps. Card payments are faster but cost more.
  7. Make Your First Deposit
    Deposit EUR via SEPA (typically free, 1-2 business days) or debit card (instant, 1.5-3% fee). Start with a small amount like €50-100 to get comfortable with the platform.
  8. Buy Your First Cryptocurrency
    Navigate to the trading section, select the EUR/crypto pair (e.g., EUR/BTC or EUR/ETH), enter the amount you want to spend, and confirm. For beginners, use "Market" orders for instant execution at current prices.
💡 Pro Tip: Start with Bitcoin (BTC) or Ethereum (ETH) for your first purchase. They have the longest track records, highest liquidity, and are available on every exchange. Avoid lesser-known "altcoins" until you understand the market better.

Essential Security Practices

Your cryptocurrency is only as secure as your practices. Follow these security essentials:

Account Security

  • Unique Password: Use a different password for your exchange account than any other service
  • Authenticator App: Use Google Authenticator, Authy, or 1Password for 2FA — never SMS alone
  • Anti-Phishing Code: Enable this feature so legitimate exchange emails are identifiable
  • Withdrawal Whitelist: Only allow withdrawals to addresses you've pre-approved
  • Device Management: Regularly review and remove old device sessions

Wallet Security

  • Small Amounts: Keep trading amounts on exchanges; larger holdings in personal wallets
  • Hardware Wallet: For amounts over €1,000, use a Ledger or Trezor device
  • Seed Phrase: Never share your 12-24 word seed phrase with anyone — not even "support"
  • Offline Storage: Write your seed phrase on paper, not digitally

Common Scams to Avoid

  • Phishing Emails: Fake emails claiming your account is locked, asking you to click a link — always verify the sender and never click suspicious links
  • Fake Support: Scammers impersonating customer support on social media — real support will never ask for your password or seed phrase
  • "Too Good" Returns: Investment schemes promising guaranteed high returns — legitimate investments never guarantee profits
  • Giveaway Scams: "Send 1 ETH, receive 2 ETH back" — these are always scams

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money do I need to start?

You can start with as little as €10-20 on most exchanges. There's no minimum requirement. It's recommended to start small while you learn, then increase your investment as you become more comfortable.

Is it legal to buy cryptocurrency in Europe?

Yes, buying, selling, and holding cryptocurrency is completely legal throughout the European Union. MiCA regulation provides a clear legal framework for crypto activities. You must, however, comply with tax reporting requirements in your country.

Which cryptocurrency should I buy first?

Most financial advisors recommend starting with Bitcoin (BTC) or Ethereum (ETH) as your first cryptocurrency. They have the longest track records, highest liquidity, and are less volatile than smaller "altcoins." Only invest in other cryptocurrencies after you understand the market better.

Do I need to pay taxes on crypto gains?

Yes, in most European countries, cryptocurrency gains are taxable. The specific treatment varies by country: Germany taxes short-term gains at income rates (tax-free after 1 year), France applies a flat 30%, and Portugal taxes gains at 28% if held under 365 days. Keep detailed records and consult a local tax professional.

Can I lose my cryptocurrency?

Yes, cryptocurrency can be lost if you lose access to your wallet or fall victim to a scam. Common ways people lose crypto: forgetting passwords without backup, sending to wrong addresses, sharing seed phrases with scammers, and exchange hacks (for amounts left on exchanges). Following security best practices minimizes these risks.

What happens if an exchange goes bankrupt?

If an exchange goes bankrupt, your funds may be at risk. This is why MiCA regulation requires exchanges to segregate customer funds. However, for amounts above €1,000, it's recommended to use a personal hardware wallet. Not your keys, not your coins.

Should I use multiple exchanges?

For beginners, mastering one exchange is sufficient. As you advance, using multiple exchanges can help you access different cryptocurrencies, compare prices, and reduce counterparty risk. Start with one, expand as needed.
