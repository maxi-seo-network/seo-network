What Are Spot Trading Fees?

Spot trading fees are the costs charged by cryptocurrency exchanges when you buy or sell digital assets on the spot market. Unlike derivatives trading, spot trading involves the immediate exchange of assets at current market prices. These fees directly impact your profitability, especially for active traders who execute multiple trades daily.

Most exchanges, including Bybit, use a maker-taker fee model. Understanding the difference between these two fee types is essential for optimizing your trading strategy.

Maker vs. Taker Fees Explained

  • Maker Fee: Charged when you place an order that doesn't execute immediately and adds liquidity to the order book. Makers typically pay lower fees because they help create market depth.
  • Taker Fee: Charged when you place an order that executes immediately against an existing order, removing liquidity from the order book. Takers pay slightly higher fees for the convenience of instant execution.

Bybit rewards market makers with lower fees, encouraging users to provide liquidity rather than just consume it. This structure benefits patient traders who use limit orders instead of market orders.
