Step-by-Step Guide for Binance

Step 1: Create an Account

  1. Go to Binance.com
  2. Click "Register" and enter your email address
  3. Create a strong, unique password (use a password manager)
  4. Verify your email address by clicking the confirmation link
  5. Enable two-factor authentication (Google Authenticator recommended)

Step 2: Complete KYC Verification

Know Your Customer (KYC) verification is required by European regulations. Here's what to expect:

  1. Go to Account → Identification
  2. Select your country of residence
  3. Upload a clear photo of your ID, passport, or driver's license
  4. Take a selfie for face verification (follow the on-screen instructions)
  5. Optionally provide proof of address if required
  6. Wait for approval (usually 10-30 minutes, sometimes up to 24 hours)

Tips for faster verification:

  • Use a well-lit area for photos
  • Ensure all document text is clearly visible
  • Take the selfie without glasses or hats
  • Use a stable internet connection

Step 3: Deposit Euros

Via SEPA Bank Transfer (Recommended - Usually Free)

SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area) transfers are the most cost-effective way to deposit EUR on Binance:

  1. Go to Wallet → Fiat and Spot
  2. Click "Deposit" next to EUR
  3. Select "Bank Transfer (SEPA)"
  4. Copy Binance's bank details carefully (IBAN, BIC, reference)
  5. Log into your bank's website or app
  6. Create a new SEPA transfer to Binance's account
  7. Enter the exact reference number provided by Binance
  8. Confirm and send (usually arrives same day or next business day)

SEPA Transfer Tips:

  • SEPA transfers within the EU are typically free from most banks
  • Processing time: Usually same day, up to 2 business days
  • Minimum deposit varies (check Binance for current limits)
  • Always include the reference number for faster crediting

Via Credit/Debit Card (Instant)

For faster purchases, you can use a credit or debit card:

  1. Go to Wallet → Fiat and Spot
  2. Click "Deposit" next to EUR
  3. Select "Card" payment method
  4. Enter your card details securely
  5. Specify the amount you want to buy
  6. Complete 3D Secure verification from your bank
  7. EUR is credited to your account within minutes

Card Purchase Considerations:

  • Higher fees: typically 1.5-3.9% depending on card and region
  • Instant availability for trading
  • Some banks may block crypto purchases - contact them if declined
  • Consider using a card that doesn't charge foreign transaction fees

Step 4: Buy Bitcoin

Once your EUR is deposited, you have several options to purchase Bitcoin:

Option A: Convert (Easiest for Beginners)

  1. Go to Trade → Convert
  2. Select EUR as "From" currency
  3. Select BTC as "To" currency
  4. Enter the amount of EUR you want to convert
  5. Click "Preview Conversion" to see the rate
  6. Click "Convert" to complete the purchase
  7. Bitcoin appears in your wallet instantly

Option B: Spot Trading (Better Rates)

  1. Go to Trade → Spot
  2. Search for the BTC/EUR trading pair
  3. Choose order type:
    • Market Order: Buy instantly at current price
    • Limit Order: Set your desired price and wait for it to fill
  4. Enter the amount of BTC you want to buy
  5. Review the order details and total cost
  6. Click "Buy BTC" to place the order
  7. For market orders, BTC appears in your wallet instantly

Option C: P2P Trading (Alternative)

Binance P2P allows you to buy BTC directly from other users:

  1. Go to Trade → P2P
  2. Select "Buy" and choose BTC
  3. Filter by EUR and your preferred payment method
  4. Choose a seller with good reputation and competitive price
  5. Enter amount and start trade
  6. Follow seller's payment instructions
  7. Mark payment as complete once done
  8. Seller releases BTC to your wallet

Spread Considerations

When using instant buy or card purchases, you may pay a spread above market price. This is the difference between the quoted price and the actual market price. For best rates:

  • Use spot trading instead of instant buy
  • Deposit via SEPA first, then trade
  • Use limit orders instead of market orders
  • Avoid peak volatility periods

Security Tips for First-Time Buyers

Account Security

  1. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Use Google Authenticator or Authy, not SMS alone. Hardware keys (YubiKey) provide the strongest protection.
  2. Use a Password Manager: Create a unique, strong password for your exchange account. Services like NordPass can help manage passwords securely.
  3. Whitelist Withdrawal Addresses: Only allow withdrawals to addresses you've pre-approved. This prevents hackers from draining your account.
  4. Enable Anti-Phishing Codes: Many exchanges offer this feature to verify legitimate emails.
  5. Bookmark Official Sites: Avoid phishing by bookmarking the official exchange URL and always check for HTTPS.

Wallet Security

Golden Rule: "Not your keys, not your coins." Keeping Bitcoin on an exchange means trusting a third party. For long-term holdings, use a hardware wallet.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Before Buying

  • Not verifying before depositing: Complete KYC verification first to avoid frozen funds. Some users deposit before verifying and can't access their funds until verification is complete.
  • Ignoring deposit limits: Check daily/monthly limits before initiating large transfers. Limits vary by verification level.
  • Using the wrong network: When depositing crypto, always use the correct blockchain network (Bitcoin network for BTC).

During Trading

  • Panic buying: Bitcoin is volatile. Don't FOMO (fear of missing out) buy at peaks. Consider dollar-cost averaging instead.
  • Ignoring the spread: Small purchases on instant buy can have unfavorable rates. For larger amounts, use spot trading.
  • Wrong trading pair: Make sure you're buying BTC/EUR, not BTC/USDT if you want to spend EUR.

After Buying

  • Leaving funds on exchange: Not your keys, not your coins. Move large amounts to a personal wallet.
  • Losing seed phrases: If you use a hardware wallet, write down your seed phrase and store it safely. Never store it digitally or share it with anyone.
  • Using weak passwords: Use a password manager to create and store strong, unique passwords.

FAQ

How fast can I buy Bitcoin with Euro?

Instant purchases with card take 5-10 minutes. Bank transfers take 1-3 business days. SEPA transfers in Europe are often same-day.

What's the cheapest way to buy Bitcoin with Euro?

Bank transfer/SEPA deposits usually have lowest fees (often free). Card purchases have higher fees (1.5-3.9%). Always compare fees on official exchange websites.

Is it safe to buy Bitcoin with my credit card?

Yes, on reputable exchanges like Binance. Use two-factor authentication and a hardware wallet for maximum security. Never share your card details on unverified platforms.

Can I buy Bitcoin without verification?

Most regulated exchanges require KYC for EUR purchases. P2P platforms may offer alternatives with limits. Always comply with local regulations. Decentralized exchanges (DEXs) don't require KYC but don't support fiat directly.

What's the minimum Bitcoin purchase?

Most exchanges allow purchases as low as €10-15 worth of Bitcoin. You don't need to buy a whole Bitcoin. Bitcoin is divisible up to 8 decimal places (satoshis).

Can I buy Bitcoin with Multibanco?

Multibanco is not directly supported by major exchanges. Use SEPA bank transfer (free, same-day to 2 days) or credit/debit card instead.

Do I pay taxes on Bitcoin purchases in Portugal?

As of 2026, Portugal does not tax individual cryptocurrency gains. However, tax laws can change. Always verify current tax treatment with official Portuguese tax authorities.

Can I lose money buying Bitcoin?

Yes. Bitcoin is volatile and prices can go down. Only invest what you can afford to lose. Consider dollar-cost averaging to reduce timing risk.
