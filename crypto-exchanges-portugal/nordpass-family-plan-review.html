Security Features: How NordPass Protects Your Family

XChaCha20 Encryption

NordPass uses XChaCha20 encryption with 256-bit keys—the same encryption standard trusted by leading security products worldwide. Unlike the more common AES-256, XChaCha20 encrypts data in streams rather than blocks, which offers several advantages:

  • Faster performance: Stream encryption works efficiently across all devices, including older smartphones
  • No hardware dependency: Doesn't require specialized chips to maintain performance
  • Enhanced nonce security: Larger nonce size reduces risks from key/nonce reuse

Zero-Knowledge Architecture

NordPass employs zero-knowledge architecture, meaning:

  • Your master password never leaves your device
  • NordPass cannot access your encrypted data
  • Even if NordPass servers were compromised, your data remains protected
  • Decryption happens locally on your devices only

Password Health Monitoring

The Password Health feature analyzes your stored credentials and identifies:

  • Weak passwords: Credentials that could be easily guessed or cracked
  • Reused passwords: Same password used across multiple accounts
  • Old passwords: Credentials that haven't been updated in an extended period

Each family member can independently monitor and improve their password hygiene, strengthening the entire household's security posture.

Data Breach Scanner

The Data Breach Scanner continuously monitors known data breaches and alerts family members when their credentials appear in compromised databases. This proactive monitoring helps families respond quickly to potential security incidents before damage occurs.

Family Sharing and Collaboration Features

Secure Password Sharing

Not all passwords need to be shared—but when families need to share Netflix credentials, Wi-Fi codes, or utility account logins, NordPass makes it secure. The sharing process uses end-to-end encryption, ensuring credentials are never transmitted in plaintext.

How it works:

  1. Select the credential you want to share
  2. Choose which family member(s) should receive access
  3. Set permissions (view-only or full access)
  4. The shared item appears in their vault with appropriate access rights

Emergency Access

What happens if a family member forgets their master password or loses access to their device? NordPass includes emergency access functionality, allowing designated family members to request access to your vault. You can set a waiting period (from hours to days), after which access is automatically granted if you don't respond—providing both security and a safety net.

Individual Private Vaults

Each of the six accounts maintains complete privacy. While passwords can be shared voluntarily, no family member can access another's vault without explicit sharing. This respects individual privacy while enabling collaboration where needed.

Beyond Passwords: Additional Premium Features

NordPass Authenticator

Two-factor authentication significantly improves account security. NordPass includes a built-in authenticator, allowing family members to generate time-based one-time passwords (TOTP) directly within the app. This eliminates the need for a separate authenticator app and keeps all security tools in one place.

Secure Notes

Store sensitive information beyond passwords: software licenses, Wi-Fi codes, membership numbers, or personal identification details. Secure notes use the same XChaCha20 encryption as passwords.

Credit Card and Identity Storage

Save payment cards and personal information for quick, secure autofill during online transactions. This feature streamlines checkout processes while maintaining security.

File Attachments

Attach important files to password entries—such as photos of membership cards, PDF documents, or recovery codes. All attachments receive the same encryption protection.

Email Masking (Where Available)

In supported regions, Email Masking creates disposable email addresses for signing up to services. This protects your primary email from spam and potential data breaches.
