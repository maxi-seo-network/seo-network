Security Features Comparison

Security is the primary reason for using a hardware wallet, and both devices take this responsibility seriously. When comparing Ledger Flex vs Trezor Safe 7, we need to examine their security architectures in detail.

Ledger Flex Security

  • Secure Element: CC EAL5+ certified chip
  • OS: Proprietary BOLOS operating system
  • Seed Protection: 24-word recovery phrase
  • Passphrase Support: Yes (25th word)
  • Physical Buttons: Two buttons for confirmation
  • tamper-proof: Yes

Trezor Safe 7 Security

  • Secure Element: EAL6+ certified chip
  • OS: Open-source firmware
  • Seed Protection: 12 or 24-word recovery phrase
  • Passphrase Support: Yes (hidden wallet)
  • Physical Buttons: Touchscreen + button combo
  • Verification: Fully auditable code

The Ledger Flex utilizes a CC EAL5+ certified secure element, which is the same high-security chip found in passports and credit cards. This chip stores your private keys in an isolated environment that cannot be extracted, even if the device is physically compromised. Ledger's proprietary BOLOS operating system provides an additional layer of security by isolating different cryptocurrency applications from each other, preventing cross-application attacks.

Trezor Safe 7 features an EAL6+ certified secure element, representing an even higher certification level. However, Trezor's approach differs significantly from Ledger's. The company maintains that their open-source firmware allows security researchers and users to verify that the device operates exactly as claimed. This transparency means that vulnerabilities can be identified and fixed quickly by the community, rather than relying solely on the manufacturer's internal security team.

Seed Phrase and Recovery

Both devices generate and store recovery seeds that allow you to restore your wallets if the device is lost or damaged. Ledger Flex uses a 24-word recovery phrase generated by the secure element, providing an astronomical number of possible combinations. The device guides you through the backup process during initial setup, ensuring you've properly recorded your seed before proceeding.

Trezor Safe 7 offers flexibility with either 12 or 24-word recovery phrases, allowing users to choose their preferred security level. The device also supports Shamir Backup, an advanced recovery method that splits your seed into multiple shares, requiring a specified number of shares to recover your wallet. This provides an additional layer of protection against partial seed compromise.

Passphrase Protection

Both devices support passphrase protection, often called the "25th word" or "hidden wallet" feature. This advanced security measure adds an optional passphrase to your recovery seed, creating a completely separate wallet that's invisible without knowing the passphrase. If someone gains access to your seed phrase but doesn't know your passphrase, they cannot access your passphrase-protected funds.
