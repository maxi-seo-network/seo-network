Security Comparison: Trezor vs Ledger

How Trezor Security Works

Trezor takes an open-source approach to security:

  • 100% open-source firmware: Every line of code is auditable by anyone
  • EAL6+ Secure Element: Safe 3 and Safe 5 include a secure chip resistant to physical attacks
  • Shamir Backup: Split your seed phrase into multiple shares for redundancy
  • Transparent verification: Device authenticity can be independently verified
  • No proprietary secrets: No closed-source components

The open-source philosophy means security researchers worldwide can audit the code, finding vulnerabilities before attackers do.

How Ledger Security Works

Ledger takes a certification-based approach:

  • CC EAL5+ certification: The highest security certification for consumer devices
  • Secure Element chip: Military-grade hardware isolation for private keys
  • Proprietary Ledger OS: Closed-source operating system protects key operations
  • Root of trust: Hardware-level verification prevents tampering
  • Recovery phrase protection: Seed never leaves the secure chip

The certification process involves extensive third-party testing, providing formal assurance of security claims.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better: Trezor or Ledger?

It depends on your priorities. Ledger offers more cryptocurrency support (5,500+) and Bluetooth connectivity. Trezor offers 100% open-source firmware and better transparency. For most beginners in Europe, both are excellent choices—choose based on whether you value convenience (Ledger) or open-source transparency (Trezor).

Is Trezor safer than Ledger?

Both are extremely secure. Ledger has CC EAL5+ security certification and a secure element chip. Trezor uses open-source firmware that can be independently audited and offers EAL6+ secure chip in newer models. Neither has ever had user funds stolen through a device vulnerability. Security depends more on how you protect your seed phrase than which device you choose.

Can Trezor hold the same coins as Ledger?

Ledger supports more cryptocurrencies (5,500+) than Trezor (1,800+). However, both support all major coins including Bitcoin, Ethereum, and popular ERC-20 tokens. If you hold obscure altcoins, check compatibility before purchasing. For most investors holding BTC, ETH, and major tokens, either device works perfectly.

Should I buy Trezor Safe 5 or Ledger Nano X?

Choose Trezor Safe 5 for open-source transparency, touchscreen interface, and Shamir backup. Choose Ledger Nano X for Bluetooth connectivity, mobile app support, and wider cryptocurrency selection. Both cost around €130-150 and offer excellent security. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize transparency (Trezor) or convenience (Ledger).

Can I use Trezor or Ledger in Portugal and Europe?

Yes, both Trezor and Ledger work perfectly in Portugal and all European countries. Both companies ship to the EU, and you can buy directly from official stores. Trezor is based in Prague (Czech Republic), making it a European company, while Ledger is based in Paris, France. Both comply with EU consumer protection laws.

What happens if I lose my Trezor or Ledger?

You can restore your funds using your seed phrase (12-24 words) on any compatible wallet. This is why writing down your seed phrase and storing it safely is the most important step. The seed phrase is your wallet—the device is just an interface. Lose the device, buy a new one, restore from seed, and your funds are safe.

Can I use both Trezor and Ledger?

Yes. You can own both devices and use them for different purposes. You can even restore the same seed phrase on both devices to access the same funds from either. Some users keep one device for daily use and another as backup. Both devices can coexist in your security setup.

Is my crypto safe on exchanges like Binance or Coinbase?

No. "Not your keys, not your coins." When crypto is on an exchange, the exchange holds your keys. They can freeze your account, be hacked, go bankrupt (like FTX), or face regulatory issues. A hardware wallet gives you sole control of your keys. For amounts over €500, a hardware wallet is strongly recommended.
