Security Features

Security is where Ledger has built its reputation, and the Nano S Plus doesn't disappoint. Let's examine the security architecture in detail:

Secure Element Chip (CC EAL6+)

At the heart of the Ledger Nano S Plus is the ST33K1M5 Secure Element chip, certified at CC EAL6+ level. This is the same security certification used in banking and government applications. The chip is specifically designed to resist:

  • Physical attacks: Tampering attempts require sophisticated equipment and expertise
  • Side-channel attacks: Timing and power analysis attacks are mitigated
  • Software vulnerabilities: Private keys never leave the Secure Element

The EAL6+ certification represents one of the highest security levels achievable for commercial products. Very few hardware wallets match this standard.

Isolated Key Storage

Your private keys are generated and stored entirely within the Secure Element chip. They never touch your computer, phone, or any internet-connected device. When you sign a transaction:

  1. The transaction data enters the device via USB
  2. The Secure Element validates and signs internally
  3. Only the signed signature leaves the device

This isolation is fundamental to cold storage security—even if your computer is compromised with malware, your private keys remain protected.

PIN Protection and Recovery

The device requires a 4-8 digit PIN for access. After 8 incorrect attempts, the device wipes itself—a security feature that protects against brute-force attacks. Your 24-word recovery phrase (BIP39 standard) allows you to restore your wallet on any compatible device if your Ledger is lost, stolen, or damaged.

Ledger OS

The device runs on Ledger OS, a purpose-built operating system designed specifically for cryptocurrency security. Unlike general-purpose operating systems, Ledger OS has a minimal attack surface and is regularly updated with security patches.

Setup Process

Setting up the Ledger Nano S Plus is straightforward, though first-time hardware wallet users should follow each step carefully. Here's the complete setup process:

Step 1: Verify Device Authenticity

Before setup, verify your device is genuine:

  • Check the holographic seal on the box is intact
  • Connect to Ledger's official Ledger Live app
  • The app will verify the device's authenticity automatically

Important: Never use a device with a broken seal or one that comes pre-configured. Always buy from Ledger's official store or authorized resellers.

Step 2: Initialize the Device

On first boot, choose between:

  • Set up as new device: Generates a fresh 24-word recovery phrase
  • Restore from recovery phrase: Import an existing wallet

For most users, setting up as new is recommended. Follow the on-screen prompts to create your PIN.

Step 3: Write Down Your Recovery Phrase

The device displays your 24-word recovery phrase one word at a time. Write these words in order on the provided recovery sheet. This is your only backup—lose this, and you lose access to your crypto permanently.

Critical security rules:

  • Never store your recovery phrase digitally (photos, cloud, screenshots)
  • Never share it with anyone, including Ledger support
  • Consider a metal backup for fire/water protection

Step 4: Install Ledger Wallet App

Download the Ledger Wallet app (formerly Ledger Live) on your computer or Android device:

  • Windows 10/11, macOS Monterey/Ventura, Ubuntu LTS 20.04/22.04
  • Android 10+ devices

Connect your device via USB-C and follow the pairing instructions.

Step 5: Install Cryptocurrency Apps

For each cryptocurrency you want to manage, install its corresponding app:

  1. Open Manager in Ledger Wallet app
  2. Find your desired cryptocurrency
  3. Click Install while your device is connected

The Nano S Plus supports up to 100 apps simultaneously—a significant improvement over the original Nano S.
