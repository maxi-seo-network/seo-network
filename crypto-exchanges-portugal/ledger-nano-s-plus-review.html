Security Features

Security is the primary reason to invest in a hardware wallet, and Ledger has built its reputation on providing bank-grade protection for digital assets.

Certified Secure Element

At the heart of the Nano S Plus lies a Secure Element chip certified to CC EAL5+ standards—the same certification level used in credit cards and passports. This tamper-resistant chip stores your private keys in an isolated environment that cannot be accessed by external software, even if your computer is compromised with malware.

The Secure Element performs all cryptographic operations internally, ensuring that your private keys never leave the device in unencrypted form. This isolation provides protection against:

  • Keyloggers: Cannot capture what you type because keys are generated and stored on the device
  • Screen scrapers: Cannot see transaction details verified on the device screen
  • Remote access trojans: Cannot extract private keys from the isolated chip
  • Clipboard hijackers: Cannot modify addresses you verify on the device display

Physical Button Verification

Every transaction requires physical confirmation using the two buttons on the device. This means remote attackers cannot authorize transactions even if they gain access to your computer. You must physically press buttons on the device to approve any outgoing transaction.

Recovery Seed Backup

During setup, the Nano S Plus generates a 24-word recovery phrase. This seed phrase allows you to restore your wallet on any compatible device if your hardware wallet is lost, stolen, or damaged. Ledger includes recovery sheets for writing down your seed phrase securely.

Important: Never store your seed phrase digitally. Write it on paper and store it in a secure location such as a safe or safety deposit box.

Passphrase Support

Advanced users can enable the passphrase feature, which adds a 25th word to the recovery phrase. This creates a hidden wallet that provides plausible deniability and an additional layer of security for those who require it.
