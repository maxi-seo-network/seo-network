Bybit Trading Fees Explained 2026: Complete Fee Structure Guide

Understanding trading fees is crucial for maximizing your crypto profits. Bybit has become one of the most popular exchanges for derivatives and spot trading, but how do their fees compare to competitors like Binance?

Bybit Spot Trading Fees

Bybit's spot trading fees follow a maker-taker model:

User Level Maker Fee Taker Fee
Regular (VIP 0) 0.10% 0.10%
VIP 1 0.08% 0.08%
VIP 2 0.06% 0.07%
VIP 3 0.04% 0.06%
VIP 4+ 0.02% 0.05%
Pro Tip: Always use limit orders when possible. You'll pay maker fees (lower) instead of taker fees. Market orders always pay taker fees.

What Are Maker and Taker Fees?

  • Maker: You add liquidity to the order book by placing a limit order that doesn't execute immediately. You "make" the market.
  • Taker: You remove liquidity by placing an order that executes immediately against an existing order. You "take" from the market.

Hidden Fees to Watch Out For

Funding Rates (Futures)

Funding rates are paid between long and short positions every 8 hours. They're not fees to Bybit, but they affect your PnL.

Spread

The bid-ask spread is technically not a fee, but it's a hidden cost. High-liquidity pairs like BTC/USDT have tight spreads.

Conversion Fees

Bybit charges fees for crypto-to-crypto conversions. Use spot trading instead for better rates.
