TradingView Free Plan: Is It Enough for Beginners?

The free plan is where everyone starts. And here's the truth: for most beginners, the free plan is completely sufficient. You get professional-grade charting tools at zero cost.

  • Charts: 1 chart per tab (can open multiple tabs)

Free Plan Limitations

While powerful, the free plan has restrictions that might frustrate active traders:

  • Single Chart View: Can only see one chart at a time. Switching between Bitcoin, Ethereum, and your altcoin trades becomes tedious.
  • Limited Indicators: 2-3 indicators maximum. If your strategy uses RSI, MACD, Volume, and Moving Averages, you'll need to drop one.
  • Only 3 Alerts: If you're tracking 10+ cryptocurrencies, constantly deleting and recreating alerts is frustrating.
  • Ads: Ads appear throughout the interface. They don't block charts but can be distracting.
  • One Saved Layout: If you switch between day trading and swing trading setups, you'll need to manually reconfigure each time.
  • No Volume Profile: Advanced tools like Volume Profile are unavailable.
  • No Bar Replay: Cannot practice on historical data bar-by-bar.

Who Should Use the Free Plan?

The free plan is ideal if you:
