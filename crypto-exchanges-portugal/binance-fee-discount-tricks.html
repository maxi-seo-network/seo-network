Trick #5: Choosing the Right Order Type

Beyond platform features, your trading behavior directly impacts fees. Understanding maker versus taker orders can save significant money over time.

Maker Orders: The Lower-Fee Strategy

When you place a limit order that doesn't execute immediately (because your price differs from the current market price), you're a "maker"—you're adding liquidity to the order book. Maker orders typically receive:

  • Lower fees than taker orders
  • At VIP levels 1+, maker fees drop dramatically (down to 0.02%)
  • Potential for negative fees (rebates) at highest VIP levels

Practical Tips for Maker-Focused Trading

  1. Use limit orders instead of market orders: Set your target price slightly below current market for buys (or above for sells)
  2. Be patient: Let the market come to your price rather than chasing it
  3. Use post-only orders: Some platforms offer "post-only" option that ensures your order never takes liquidity
  4. Scale into positions: Place multiple limit orders at different price levels

This strategy works best for position building rather than quick entries. European traders with longer time horizons can benefit significantly from maker-focused approaches.
