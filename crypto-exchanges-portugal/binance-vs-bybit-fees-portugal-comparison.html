Both exchanges use the maker-taker fee model:

  • Maker fee: Charged when you place a limit order that adds liquidity to the order book (not immediately filled)
  • Taker fee: Charged when you place an order that removes liquidity (market orders or limit orders filled immediately)

Hidden Fees and Costs to Watch

Beyond advertised trading fees, be aware of these potential costs:

1. Spread Markup

The "spread" is the difference between buy and sell prices. Both exchanges may have different spreads on different trading pairs. EUR pairs may have slightly wider spreads than USDT pairs due to lower liquidity, but this is often offset by avoiding conversion fees.

2. Conversion Fees

If you use Binance's "Convert" feature or Bybit's equivalent for quick trades, you may pay higher spreads than on the spot market. Always compare the convert rate with the spot market rate.

3. Network Fees (Not Exchange Fees)

Network fees for withdrawals go to miners/validators, not the exchange. Both exchanges pass these fees through to users. Network congestion can significantly increase these fees.

4. Inactivity Fees

Neither Binance nor Bybit charges inactivity fees, so your account won't be charged for not trading.
