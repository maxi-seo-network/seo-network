Security Deep Dive: Identical Protection, Different Packages

Both the Trezor Safe 3 and Trezor Safe 5 share identical core security features. This is crucial to understand: you're not sacrificing security by choosing the cheaper model.

Shared Security Features

  • EAL6+ Secure Element: The highest certification level available for consumer devices, protecting against physical tampering and side-channel attacks
  • Open-source firmware: Full transparency with code that anyone can audit
  • On-device verification: All transaction confirmations happen on the device, never on your potentially compromised computer
  • PIN and passphrase entry on device: Even if your computer has a keylogger, your credentials remain safe
  • FIDO2 authentication: Use either device as a hardware 2FA key for online accounts
  • Tor support: Route your Trezor Suite connection through Tor for enhanced privacy
  • Coin control: Advanced privacy features for Bitcoin and compatible coins

Backup and Recovery

Both devices support the new 20-word backup standard, which improves upon the traditional 12 and 24-word BIP39 format. This universal standard allows seamless transitions between different backup schemes:

  • Single-share Backup: Standard recovery with one set of words
  • Multi-share Backup: Split your seed into multiple shares—you can require any combination to recover (e.g., 3 of 5 shares)

The Multi-share Backup feature is particularly valuable for estate planning or distributed trust scenarios, ensuring your heirs or trusted contacts can recover your assets without exposing your complete seed to any single party.

User Experience Comparison

Safe 3: You'll scroll through transaction details using buttons, pressing both to confirm. The OLED screen shows essential information clearly, but you're limited to text-based displays.

Safe 5: The touchscreen lets you swipe through details and tap to confirm. The color display can show more information at once, and the larger screen makes address verification easier—particularly important for preventing man-in-the-middle attacks.

Firmware Updates

Both devices update through Trezor Suite, but the Safe 5's larger display shows more progress information. The update process is equally secure on both devices.

Mobile Usage

Both devices connect via USB-C and work with Trezor Suite mobile app on Android. Neither supports full functionality on iOS due to Apple's restrictions, but you can verify transactions on both platforms.
