How to Calculate Crypto Taxes

Example 1: Short-term Trading

Scenario: You bought €10,000 worth of Bitcoin in March 2026 and sold it in November 2026 for €15,000.

Calculation:

  • Holding period: 8 months (<365 days)
  • Gain: €15,000 - €10,000 = €5,000
  • Tax: €5,000 × 28% = €1,400

Example 2: Long-term Holding

Scenario: You bought €10,000 worth of Bitcoin in January 2024 and sold it in January 2026 for €25,000.

Calculation:

  • Holding period: 2 years (≥365 days)
  • Gain: €25,000 - €10,000 = €15,000
  • Tax: €0 (tax-free)

How to Report Crypto Taxes in Portugal

  1. Keep Records
    Track all transactions with dates, amounts, and values in EUR. Use a crypto tax calculator or exchange history.
  2. Calculate Gains
    Determine which gains are short-term (taxable) vs long-term (tax-free).
  3. Complete IRS Annex G
    Report taxable gains in Annex G (rendimentos de capitais) of your IRS tax return.
  4. Pay by April 30
    Tax returns are due by April 30 for the previous tax year.
  5. Keep Documentation
    Keep exchange statements for 10 years in case of audit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is crypto legal in Portugal?

Yes, cryptocurrency is legal in Portugal. You can buy, sell, hold, and trade crypto. The government has introduced taxation rules since 2023.

Do I need to report crypto even if I didn't sell?

No. Only realized gains (when you sell crypto for fiat or other assets) need to be reported. Unrealized gains are not taxed.

What happens if I don't report crypto taxes?

Failure to report can result in penalties of 10% to 100% of the tax owed, plus interest. The statute of limitations is 10 years.

Does Portugal tax staking rewards?

Yes, staking rewards are taxed as income at 28%. You must report them in the year they are received.

Can I deduct crypto losses in Portugal?

Short-term losses can offset short-term gains. You cannot use short-term losses to offset other income.

Is DeFi taxed in Portugal?

Yes, DeFi yields from lending, liquidity mining, and similar activities are taxed at 28% as income.

What about NFT taxes in Portugal?

NFTs are treated similarly to crypto. Short-term gains are taxed at 28%, long-term gains are tax-free.
