Frequently Asked Questions

Is Koinly safe to use?

Koinly uses read-only API connections, meaning it cannot execute trades or withdraw funds from your accounts. The platform does not store your private keys or have access to your funds. Your exchange API keys remain under your control and can be revoked at any time. However, as with any financial software, use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication on your account.

Can Koinly handle crypto-to-crypto trades?

Yes, Koinly automatically calculates gains and losses for crypto-to-crypto trades. It determines the fair market value at the time of each trade in your base currency (EUR, USD, etc.) and tracks the cost basis across multiple trades.

Does Koinly support staking rewards?

Yes, Koinly tracks staking rewards as income. It imports staking transactions from supported exchanges and blockchain wallets, calculating the fair market value at the time of receipt for tax purposes.

What happens if I can't find my exchange on Koinly?

If your exchange isn't directly supported, you can usually export a CSV file from your exchange and import it into Koinly. The platform provides CSV import templates and guides for formatting your data correctly.

Can I use Koinly for multiple tax years?

Yes, Koinly allows you to generate tax reports for previous years. You can import historical transactions and generate reports for any tax year, though you typically need to purchase a separate report for each year.

Does Koinly guarantee my tax report is correct?

No, Koinly provides a tool to calculate your taxes, but you remain responsible for the accuracy of your tax filing. The software relies on the data you import—if transactions are missing or incorrect, your report will be too. Always review your data carefully and consider consulting with a tax professional, especially for complex situations.
