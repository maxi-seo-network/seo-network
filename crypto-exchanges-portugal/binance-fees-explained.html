Binance Trading Fees Overview

Binance employs a maker-taker fee model common across cryptocurrency exchanges. Understanding this distinction is crucial because it directly affects what you pay on each transaction.

Maker vs Taker: What's the Difference?

Maker fees apply when you place a limit order that doesn't execute immediately. Your order goes into the order book, adding liquidity to the market. You "make" the market by providing trading opportunities for others.

Taker fees apply when your order executes immediately against an existing order in the book. You "take" liquidity by removing available trading opportunities. Market orders always incur taker fees.

This system rewards liquidity providers with lower fees, which helps maintain a healthy trading ecosystem.
