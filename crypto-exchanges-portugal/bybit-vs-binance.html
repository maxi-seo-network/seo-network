Features Comparison

Spot Trading

Binance offers the widest selection of spot trading pairs globally, with over 350 cryptocurrencies and hundreds of trading pairs. The platform supports advanced order types including limit, market, stop-limit, OCO (One-Cancels-the-Other), and trailing stop orders.

Bybit provides spot trading for major cryptocurrencies with a more curated selection. While it has expanded its offerings significantly, it still trails Binance in total pairs available. However, the spot trading interface is considered more intuitive by many users.

Futures & Derivatives

This is where Bybit truly shines. The exchange specializes in derivatives and offers:

  • USDT-margined perpetual contracts

  • Quarterly futures
  • Up to 100x leverage on major pairs
  • Advanced risk management tools

Binance also offers comprehensive futures products with similar leverage options, but Bybit's derivatives interface is specifically designed for professional traders with advanced charting capabilities and faster execution.

Security Comparison

Track Record

Binance has operated since 2017 and has weathered several security incidents over the years. The most notable was a 2019 hack where hackers stole over $40 million in Bitcoin. Binance responded by using its Secure Asset Fund for Users (SAFU) to cover the losses, ensuring no user funds were affected.

Bybit was founded in 2018 and has maintained a clean security record with no major hacks to date. This is a significant achievement for an exchange handling billions in daily volume.

Regulatory Compliance

Binance has faced increased regulatory scrutiny globally. The exchange has worked to improve compliance, obtaining registrations in various jurisdictions and strengthening its Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures. Binance.US was created specifically to comply with US regulations.

Bybit has adopted a more conservative regulatory approach, avoiding certain markets entirely (including the United States) to minimize compliance risks. This has resulted in fewer regulatory issues but also limited accessibility.

Winner: Both exchanges take security seriously. Binance has a more established track record and larger insurance fund, while Bybit's clean record and conservative approach appeal to risk-averse traders.

Bybit's interface is notably cleaner and more streamlined. The trading dashboard is intuitive, with clear visualization of positions, orders, and market data. Many traders prefer Bybit's layout for derivatives trading specifically.

Supported Countries

  • Binance.com: Serves most countries globally (180+)
  • Binance.US: Specifically for United States users
  • Binance Square: Regional services in select markets

However, Binance is not available in several jurisdictions due to regulatory restrictions, including:

  • United States (must use Binance.US)
  • China (mainland)
  • Malaysia (partially restricted)
  • Ontario, Canada
  • Japan

Bybit Global Availability

Bybit serves users in over 160 countries but has explicitly exited certain markets:

  • Not available in: United States, Quebec (Canada), Singapore, Hong Kong, Cuba, North Korea, Crimea, Sevastopol, Iran, Syria, Sudan

Good news for Portuguese traders: Both Binance and Bybit are accessible from Portugal. Neither platform has restricted Portuguese users, and both offer services in Portuguese. Portuguese-speaking traders will find customer support and interface options available in their language.

Winner: Binance has broader global availability with regional entities. Bybit has exited more high-profile markets but covers most European countries including Portugal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bybit safer than Binance?

Both exchanges prioritize security with industry-standard practices. Binance has a larger security fund (SAFU, over $1 billion) and a longer operational track record. Bybit has maintained a clean record with no major hacks since its founding in 2018. Both are considered highly secure, but Binance's insurance fund provides an additional layer of protection.

Which exchange has lower fees: Bybit or Binance?

Both offer competitive maker/taker fees starting at 0.10%/0.10%. Binance has more aggressive volume-based discounts that can reduce fees to 0.02%/0.04% for high-volume traders. Bybit offers similar tiered structures with occasional zero-fee promotions on specific pairs. For casual traders, fees are nearly identical.

Can US citizens use Bybit or Binance?

Binance.US is available for US citizens with restricted features and a limited selection of cryptocurrencies. Bybit does not serve US citizens and has fully exited the US market. US residents should use Binance.US or alternative exchanges like Kraken or Coinbase.

Which exchange has better liquidity?

Binance has significantly higher trading volume and liquidity across most trading pairs. This translates to tighter spreads and better execution for large orders. Bybit ranks second or third globally in derivatives volume and still offers excellent liquidity for major pairs like BTC and ETH.

Which is better for beginners: Bybit or Binance?

Binance is generally better for beginners due to its more intuitive basic interface, extensive educational resources (Binance Academy), and broader range of supported countries. Bybit has improved its onboarding process but is often preferred by more experienced traders who value its streamlined derivatives trading experience.

Both Bybit and Binance are excellent cryptocurrency exchanges that serve different trader profiles.
