Security Best Practices for Crypto Password Management

1. Never Store Passwords in Plain Text

Browser password managers, Excel files, and notes apps are not secure. If malware infects your device, these are the first things attackers target. Always use a dedicated, encrypted password manager.

2. Use Unique Passwords for Every Account

Credential stuffing attacks are automated—hackers use leaked passwords from one breach to try logging into hundreds of services. If you use the same password on a forum and a crypto exchange, your funds are at risk.

3. Enable Biometric Login

Biometric authentication (fingerprint, Face ID) is both more convenient and more secure than typing passwords. It prevents keylogger attacks and shoulder surfing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Using Simple Master Passwords

Your master password must be uncrackable. A simple password undermines everything. Use a passphrase—a sequence of random words that's easy to remember but impossible to guess. Example: "correct-horse-battery-staple-rocket" is stronger than "P@ssw0rd123".

Mistake #2: Storing Master Passwords Digitally

Never save your master password in a document, email draft, or notes app. If malware gets on your device, these will be found. Write it on paper and store it securely.

Mistake #3: Ignoring Security Alerts

When NordPass alerts you to a weak password or data breach, act immediately. Attackers often act within hours of a breach becoming public.

Mistake #4: Skipping 2FA

Even the strongest password can be compromised through phishing or keylogging. 2FA is your backup—and for crypto accounts, essential.

Mistake #5: Using SMS-Based 2FA

SMS 2FA is vulnerable to SIM-swapping attacks. Use authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy) or hardware keys (YubiKey) instead.
