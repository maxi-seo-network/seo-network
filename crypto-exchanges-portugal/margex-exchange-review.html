Leverage Trading on Margex

Leverage is Margex's flagship feature, allowing traders to control larger positions with smaller capital. This powerful tool amplifies both potential profits and potential losses, making it essential to understand before trading.

How Leverage Works

When trading with leverage, you only need to provide a fraction of the total position value as margin. The exchange essentially lends you the remaining capital to open a larger position than your actual balance would allow:

  • With 10x leverage, $100 margin controls a $1,000 position. A 10% price move in your favor doubles your margin, but a 10% move against you liquidates the position.
  • With 50x leverage, $100 margin controls a $5,000 position. A 2% price move in your favor doubles your margin, but a 2% move against you liquidates the position.
  • With 100x leverage, $100 margin controls a $10,000 position. A 1% price move in your favor doubles your margin, but a 1% move against you liquidates the position.

As you can see, higher leverage provides greater profit potential but significantly reduces the margin for error. This is why experienced traders often recommend beginners start with lower leverage (5x-10x) until they fully understand the mechanics and risks.

⚠️ Understanding Liquidation Risk

Higher leverage means smaller margin of safety. With 100x leverage, a 1% adverse price move can liquidate your entire position. Always use stop-loss orders and proper risk management. Consider starting with 5x-10x leverage until experienced.

Margin Types

  • Isolated Margin - Each position has dedicated margin. If liquidated, only that position's margin is lost. Recommended for beginners.
  • Cross Margin - All positions share your account balance as margin. More buffer but risks entire balance.
