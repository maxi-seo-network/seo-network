Security Risks of Trading Without a VPN

IP Address Exposure

Every Binance login records your IP address. This reveals your approximate location, ISP, and creates a digital footprint that can be traced back to you. For traders moving significant capital, this information is valuable to malicious actors.

ISP Surveillance

Your internet service provider can see which domains you visit. While HTTPS encrypts transaction details, your ISP knows you're actively trading cryptocurrency. This metadata can be sold, requested by authorities, or used to profile your financial activity.

Public WiFi Vulnerabilities

Trading while traveling or at coffee shops exposes you to man-in-the-middle attacks. Attackers on public networks can intercept unencrypted traffic, inject malicious code, or steal session cookies to gain unauthorized access to your accounts.

Correlation Attacks

Sophisticated adversaries can correlate IP addresses, trading times, and blockchain transactions to deanonymize traders. If you're moving substantial funds, this correlation risk becomes a real concern.
