What Is Wave Accounting?

Wave Accounting is a genuinely free accounting platform designed for freelancers, sole proprietors, and very small businesses. Founded in 2009 and acquired by H&R Block in 2019, Wave has become the go-to choice for budget-conscious freelancers who need basic accounting without monthly subscription fees.

Unlike competitors that offer "free trials" or "free tiers" with significant limitations, Wave provides its core accounting features at no cost—forever. The platform makes money through payment processing fees and optional paid services like payroll.

When Wave Is the Right Choice

Choose Wave if you:

  • Want to pay $0 for accounting software
  • Have simple invoicing needs (no complex projects)
  • Don't need tax estimation (you use a CPA or do taxes manually)
  • Track mileage separately (or don't drive for business)
  • Don't bill by the hour (no time tracking needed)
  • Are just starting out and watching every dollar
  • Work with an accountant who can use Wave

Choose a paid alternative if you:

  • Need tax estimation: QuickBooks Self-Employed ($15/month)
  • Need mileage tracking: QuickBooks Self-Employed ($15/month)
  • Need time tracking: FreshBooks ($15-25/month)
  • Need project profitability: FreshBooks Plus ($25/month)
  • Need inventory management: QuickBooks Online ($30+/month)

Getting Started with Wave

Step 1: Create Your Account (5 minutes)

  1. Go to waveapps.com and click "Create Free Account"
  2. Enter your email and create a password
  3. Verify your email address
  4. Add your business name and details

Step 2: Connect Bank Accounts (10 minutes)

  1. Navigate to Banking → Connect Account
  2. Search for your bank and log in securely
  3. Select which accounts to import (business only)
  4. Review imported transactions

Step 3: Customize Your Invoice (10 minutes)

  1. Go to Settings → Invoice Customization
  2. Upload your logo
  3. Choose colors that match your brand
  4. Add default payment terms
  5. Enable online payments if desired

Step 4: Send Your First Invoice (10 minutes)

  1. Go to Invoices → Create Invoice
  2. Add client details
  3. Add line items and descriptions
  4. Set due date and payment terms
  5. Send via email or share link

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Wave Accounting really free?

Yes, Wave Accounting core features are completely free. You can send unlimited invoices, track unlimited expenses, connect unlimited bank accounts, and generate financial reports at no cost. Wave makes money through payment processing fees (2.9% + 30¢ per transaction) and optional paid add-ons like payroll.

What's the catch with Wave free accounting?

Wave's 'catch' is that advanced features require payment: payroll ($40/month + $6/employee), bookkeeping support ($18/month), and professional invoice templates (paid add-on). Additionally, customer support is primarily email-based with slower response times than paid alternatives. The software also lacks inventory management and project profitability tracking.

Is Wave good for freelancers?

Yes, Wave is excellent for freelancers with simple accounting needs. The free core features cover invoicing, expense tracking, and basic financial reporting. However, freelancers who need tax estimation, mileage tracking, or project profitability tracking should consider paid alternatives like QuickBooks Self-Employed or FreshBooks.

Does Wave work for sole proprietors?

Yes, Wave is designed for sole proprietors, freelancers, and independent contractors. The platform supports Schedule C reporting and simple business structures. If your business grows to include employees or complex inventory, you may need to upgrade to paid accounting software.

How does Wave make money if it's free?

Wave generates revenue through: (1) Payment processing fees - 2.9% + 30¢ when clients pay invoices via credit card, (2) Payroll services - $40/month + $6/employee for businesses with employees, (3) Bookkeeping support - $18/month for access to accounting experts, and (4) Bank partners - referral fees from financial products.

Final Verdict

🎯 Recommendation

Use Wave if: You're a budget-conscious freelancer who needs basic invoicing and expense tracking. The free features are genuinely free and cover most needs.

Skip Wave if: You need tax estimation, mileage tracking, time tracking, or project profitability. QuickBooks Self-Employed or FreshBooks will serve you better.

Bottom line: Wave is the best free accounting option for freelancers. Period. If you don't need advanced features like tax estimation or mileage tracking, there's no reason to pay $15-50/month for alternatives. Start with Wave, and only upgrade if you hit its limitations.
