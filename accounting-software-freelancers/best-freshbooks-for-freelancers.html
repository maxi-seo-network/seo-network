Why FreshBooks Is Built for Freelancers

FreshBooks started in 2003 as a simple invoicing tool and has grown into a comprehensive accounting solution designed specifically for service-based businesses. Unlike QuickBooks (which adapted from general small business accounting), FreshBooks was built from the ground up with freelancers and agencies in mind.

The result? An interface that feels intuitive from day one, invoicing that clients actually pay promptly, and time tracking that integrates seamlessly with project billing. If you're a freelancer who bills by the hour or needs professional invoices, FreshBooks deserves serious consideration.

FreshBooks Lite: Entry Level for Solo Freelancers

What's Included

  • 5 Billable Clients: Invoice up to 5 unique clients per month
  • Unlimited Invoices: Send as many invoices as needed to those 5 clients
  • Expense Tracking: Categorize and track all business expenses
  • Time Tracking: Log hours manually or use the timer
  • Mobile App: Full-featured iOS and Android apps
  • Reports: Basic profit & loss, expense reports

What's Missing (Important!)

⚠️ Critical Limitations of Lite Plan:
  • No accountant access - Your CPA cannot log in to review your books
  • No bank reconciliation - Cannot match transactions to bank statements
  • No electronic signatures - Can't get contracts signed within FreshBooks
  • 5-client limit - Most freelancers outgrow this in 3-6 months

When Lite Makes Sense

The Lite plan works for freelancers who:

  • Have fewer than 5 recurring clients
  • Don't work with an accountant regularly
  • Need basic invoicing without bells and whistles
  • Are just starting and want to minimize costs

FreshBooks Plus: The Sweet Spot for Most Freelancers

What's Included (Everything in Lite, plus)

  • 50 Billable Clients: Invoice up to 50 unique clients
  • Accountant Access: Invite your CPA to collaborate
  • Bank Reconciliation: Match transactions to statements
  • Electronic Signatures: Get contracts signed within FreshBooks
  • Double-Entry Accounting: Full accounting functionality
  • Project Profitability: See which projects make money
  • Advanced Reports: More detailed financial insights

Why Plus Is Worth the Extra $10/month

The jump from Lite ($15) to Plus ($25) is where FreshBooks becomes a serious accounting tool. Here's what that $10 gets you:

  • Accountant collaboration: Your CPA can access your books remotely
  • 10x more clients: From 5 to 50 billable clients
  • Bank reconciliation: Essential for accurate bookkeeping
  • E-signatures: Save $15-30/month on separate signature software
💡 Our Recommendation: Most freelancers should start with Plus, not Lite. The 5-client limit on Lite is too restrictive, and you'll likely need accountant access eventually. Starting on Plus avoids migration headaches later.

FreshBooks Premium: For Growing Freelance Businesses

What's Included (Everything in Plus, plus)

  • Unlimited Billable Clients: No client caps, ever
  • Advanced Payments: Accept credit cards with lower fees
  • Project Tracking: Detailed profitability by project
  • Team Members: Add additional users at $10/user/month
  • Custom Email Templates: Professional client communications
  • Priority Support: Faster response times

When Premium Makes Sense

Consider Premium ($50/month) if you:

  • Have more than 50 clients you invoice regularly
  • Work with contractors or virtual assistants
  • Want detailed project profitability tracking
  • Need lower credit card processing fees

Getting Started: Setup Guide

Step 1: Start Your Free Trial (5 minutes)

  1. Go to FreshBooks.com and click "Start Free Trial"
  2. No credit card required for 30-day trial
  3. Choose Plus plan (recommended) to test all features

Step 2: Set Up Your Profile (10 minutes)

  1. Add your business name and logo
  2. Customize invoice template with your branding
  3. Set up payment options (FreshBooks Payments, Stripe, etc.)

Step 3: Connect Bank Accounts (10 minutes)

  1. Link your business bank account
  2. Link your business credit card
  3. Review and categorize imported transactions

Step 4: Create Your First Invoice (15 minutes)

  1. Go to Invoices → Create Invoice
  2. Add client details
  3. Add line items (hours, fixed price, or both)
  4. Customize payment terms and due date
  5. Send via email or share link

Frequently Asked Questions

Is FreshBooks good for freelancers?

Yes, FreshBooks is excellent for freelancers because it was originally designed for service-based small businesses. The interface is intuitive, invoicing is streamlined, and time tracking integrates directly with projects. FreshBooks is particularly strong for freelancers who bill hourly and need professional-looking invoices.

Which FreshBooks plan is best for freelancers?

For most freelancers, the Plus plan at $25/month offers the best value. It includes 50 billable clients, unlimited invoices, time tracking, and expense tracking. The Lite plan ($15/month) limits you to 5 billable clients, which is too restrictive for growing freelancers.

Does FreshBooks have a free plan?

FreshBooks offers a free trial (no credit card required) but no permanently free plan. For free accounting software, Wave Accounting offers similar features at $0. However, FreshBooks' invoicing and time tracking capabilities are more polished than Wave's.

Can FreshBooks track mileage for freelancers?

Yes, FreshBooks includes mileage tracking in all plans. You can log mileage manually or use the mobile app to track trips automatically. Mileage expenses can be categorized to projects for accurate billing.

What's the difference between FreshBooks Lite and Plus?

FreshBooks Lite ($15/month) allows only 5 billable clients and excludes accountant access, bank reconciliation, and electronic signatures. FreshBooks Plus ($25/month) allows 50 billable clients and includes all core features. Most freelancers outgrow Lite within 3-6 months.

Final Verdict

🎯 Recommendations by Freelancer Type

New Freelancers (0-5 clients): Start with FreshBooks Lite at $15/month during your free trial. Upgrade to Plus before you hit the 5-client limit.

Established Freelancers (5-50 clients): Choose FreshBooks Plus at $25/month. The accountant access and bank reconciliation are worth the extra $10.

Freelancers with Teams: Choose FreshBooks Premium at $50/month. Add team members at $10/month each.

Freelancers Focused on Taxes: Consider QuickBooks Self-Employed instead. Its tax estimation features are superior.

Bottom line: FreshBooks is the best invoicing and time-tracking solution for service-based freelancers. If you bill hourly or need professional invoices that get paid faster, FreshBooks delivers. Just avoid the Lite plan—the 5-client limit is too restrictive for most growing freelancers.
