Best Value by Budget

Free Option

Wave offers the best free accounting for freelancers. Includes unlimited invoicing, expense tracking, and receipt scanning at no cost.

Under $20/month

QuickBooks Self-Employed at $15/mo is ideal for solo freelancers who need tax estimation and mileage tracking.

$20-40/month

FreshBooks Plus at $33/mo offers unlimited invoicing and project tracking. Good for service-based freelancers with multiple projects.

$40+/month

Xero Premium at $54/mo or QuickBooks Online Plus for complex businesses with inventory, multiple users, or international operations.

Hidden Costs to Consider

  • Payment processing: 2.9% + $0.30-0.60 per transaction
  • Payroll: Usually $30-50/mo + per-employee fees
  • Add-ons: Advanced features often cost extra
  • Training: Professional setup help may cost $100-500
  • Data migration: Moving from another system may require help

Money-Saving Tips

  1. Start free: Use Wave until you outgrow it
  2. Pay annually: Save 15-33% on annual plans
  3. Bundle services: QuickBooks + TurboTax bundle saves money
  4. Check for discounts: Many offer 50% off first 3-6 months
  5. Right-size your plan: Don't overpay for features you won't use

FAQ

Which accounting software is cheapest for freelancers?

Wave is completely free for basic accounting, invoicing, and receipt scanning. For paid options, QuickBooks Self-Employed starts at $15/month.

Is paid accounting software worth it?

If you're earning consistently and have multiple clients, yes. Paid software offers tax estimation, better reporting, and integrations that save hours each month.

Can I deduct accounting software?

Yes, accounting software subscriptions are fully deductible business expenses. Keep receipts of your payments.
