QuickBooks vs FreshBooks for Freelancers 2026: Which Is Better?

Complete comparison of the two most popular accounting platforms for freelancers

Quick Comparison

Best for Taxes: QuickBooks Self-Employed ($15/month)

Best for Invoicing: FreshBooks Plus ($25/month)

Best for Time Tracking: FreshBooks ($15-50/month)

Best for Mileage: QuickBooks Self-Employed ($15/month)

Best Value Overall: Depends on your primary need (see below)

The Main Difference: Tax Focus vs. Invoice Focus

QuickBooks and FreshBooks take fundamentally different approaches to serving freelancers:

QuickBooks Self-Employed

Focus: Taxes and deductions

Best for: Freelancers who want to minimize tax anxiety

  • Automatic quarterly tax estimation
  • Schedule C export
  • GPS mileage tracking
  • Business/personal expense separation

The pitch: "Never worry about quarterly taxes again."

FreshBooks

Focus: Invoicing and getting paid

Best for: Freelancers who want to look professional

  • Beautiful, customizable invoices
  • Built-in time tracking
  • Late payment reminders
  • Client portal for payments

The pitch: "Send invoices clients actually pay."

Who Should Choose QuickBooks Self-Employed?

Choose QuickBooks Self-Employed if you:

  • Want automatic quarterly tax estimates
  • Drive for business and need mileage tracking
  • File Schedule C and want easy export
  • Need to separate business and personal expenses
  • Plan to use TurboTax for filing
  • Have more than 5 clients (FreshBooks Lite limit)
  • Don't need time tracking or advanced invoicing

The Tax Advantage

QuickBooks Self-Employed's killer feature is tax estimation. If you've ever been surprised by a quarterly tax bill, this feature alone pays for the subscription. The automatic mileage tracking using GPS is also valuable—the IRS mileage deduction (67¢/mile in 2026) adds up quickly.

Who Should Choose FreshBooks?

Choose FreshBooks if you:

  • Bill by the hour and need time tracking
  • Send many invoices and want them to look professional
  • Need automatic late payment reminders
  • Work with accountants who need access
  • Have clients who pay late (FreshBooks' reminders help)
  • Need project profitability tracking
  • Don't drive much for business

The Invoice Advantage

FreshBooks' invoicing is best-in-class. Clients are more likely to pay invoices that look professional and offer easy payment options. If cash flow is your pain point (clients paying late), FreshBooks can help reduce payment delays.

Can You Use Both?

Some freelancers consider using both: QuickBooks for taxes and FreshBooks for invoicing. While technically possible, we don't recommend it because:

  • Double entry: You'll enter transactions twice
  • Confusion: Which system is the "source of truth"?
  • Cost: You're paying $30-65/month for both
  • Sync issues: Data gets out of sync quickly

💡 Better Alternative: Use Add-Ons

Instead of using both platforms, use one and supplement with add-ons:

  • QuickBooks + Time Tracking App: Use QuickBooks Self-Employed for taxes, plus a time tracking app like Toggl ($10/month)
  • FreshBooks + Mileage App: Use FreshBooks for invoicing, plus MileIQ ($5/month) for mileage

Final Verdict: QuickBooks vs FreshBooks

🎯 Our Recommendation

Choose QuickBooks Self-Employed if taxes are your pain point. The automatic quarterly tax estimation, Schedule C export, and mileage tracking make tax time significantly less stressful. $15/month.

Choose FreshBooks Plus if invoicing is your pain point. Professional invoices, time tracking that auto-populates invoices, and late payment reminders help you get paid faster. $25/month.

Choose QuickBooks Online Simple Start if you work with an accountant. Accountant access is included, and you can scale up as your business grows. $30/month.

Migration: Can You Switch Later?

QuickBooks to FreshBooks: Yes, but manual. Export transactions as CSV, import into FreshBooks. Budget 2-4 hours.

FreshBooks to QuickBooks: Same process—export and import. Note that QuickBooks Self-Employed is a separate product from QuickBooks Online. You cannot "upgrade" from Self-Employed to Online; you must start fresh.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is QuickBooks or FreshBooks better for freelancers?

It depends on your priorities. QuickBooks Self-Employed is better for freelancers who want tax estimation, mileage tracking, and Schedule C reporting. FreshBooks is better for freelancers who prioritize invoicing, time tracking, and project management. If taxes are your pain point, choose QuickBooks. If invoicing is your pain point, choose FreshBooks.

Can I switch from QuickBooks Self-Employed to FreshBooks?

Yes, you can switch, but there's no automatic migration. You'll need to export your data from QuickBooks (income, expenses, invoices) and manually import or recreate it in FreshBooks. Both platforms allow CSV exports. Budget 2-4 hours for the migration depending on your data volume.

Which is cheaper: QuickBooks or FreshBooks?

Both start at $15/month. QuickBooks Self-Employed is $15/month (unlimited clients). FreshBooks Lite is also $15/month but limits you to 5 billable clients. For a fair comparison, FreshBooks Plus ($25/month) vs QuickBooks Online Simple Start ($30/month) makes FreshBooks slightly cheaper, but QuickBooks includes mileage tracking which FreshBooks lacks on all tiers.

Does FreshBooks do taxes like QuickBooks?

No, FreshBooks does not include tax estimation or Schedule C reporting. QuickBooks Self-Employed automatically calculates quarterly tax estimates and exports Schedule C data. FreshBooks tracks income and expenses but you'll need separate software or an accountant for tax preparation.

Can I use both QuickBooks and FreshBooks together?

Technically yes, but it's not recommended. Using both creates duplicate work and confusion. Choose one system and commit to it. If you need features from both, consider upgrading to QuickBooks Online Plus (for project tracking) or using FreshBooks with a separate mileage tracking app.
