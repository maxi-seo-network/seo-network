Detailed CRM Pricing Breakdown

HubSpot CRM Pricing

HubSpot offers a generous free plan with paid tiers for advanced features:

  • Free CRM: Unlimited users, basic features, 1M contacts
  • Starter ($15/user/mo): Simple automation, removal of HubSpot branding
  • Professional ($20/user/mo): Marketing automation, custom reporting
  • Enterprise ($120/user/mo): Advanced automation, custom objects, sandboxes

Note: HubSpot's pricing is complex. Some features require purchasing "Hubs" (Marketing, Sales, Service) separately.

Salesforce Pricing

Salesforce pricing is per user with significant feature jumps between tiers:

  • Essentials ($25/user/mo): Basic CRM, up to 2 users
  • Professional ($75/user/mo): Full CRM, workflow automation
  • Enterprise ($150/user/mo): Advanced customization, API access
  • Unlimited ($300/user/mo): Full suite, unlimited customizations

Warning: Salesforce implementation often requires consultants ($5,000-50,000+).

Pipedrive Pricing

Pipedrive has straightforward pricing with no free plan:

  • Essential ($15/user/mo): Basic pipeline
  • Advanced ($29/user/mo): Email tracking, custom fields
  • Professional ($44/user/mo): Advanced reporting, team features
  • Enterprise ($99/user/mo): Unlimited features, dedicated support

Zoho CRM Pricing

Zoho offers the best value for budget-conscious agencies:

  • Free: Up to 3 users, basic features
  • Standard ($14/user/mo): Rules, custom reports
  • Professional ($40/user/mo): Automation, Canvas
  • Enterprise ($52/user/mo): Advanced customization

Hidden Costs to Consider

Implementation Costs

  • Self-setup: Free, but takes 5-20 hours
  • Consultant setup: $1,000-10,000 for complex systems
  • Salesforce implementation: Often $5,000-50,000+

Add-on Costs

  • Additional contacts: Most CRMs charge for contact limits above certain thresholds
  • Email sends: Marketing automation emails may cost extra
  • Storage: File storage limits may require upgrades
  • Integrations: Some integrations require paid add-ons
  • Training: Professional training costs $500-5,000

ROI Considerations

A CRM should pay for itself through:

  • Faster deal closings (average 15% improvement)
  • Reduced client churn (average 10% improvement)
  • Saved time (2-5 hours/week per user)
  • Better pipeline visibility and forecasting

Pricing FAQs

How much does CRM cost per user for agencies?

CRM costs range from free (HubSpot, Zoho free tiers) to $100+ per user per month. Most agencies pay $30-80 per user monthly. Expect $150-500/month for a 5-person agency depending on features needed.

What is the cheapest CRM for agencies?

HubSpot and Zoho both offer free CRM plans. Zoho allows up to 3 users free. HubSpot's free tier is more feature-rich but requires paid plans for advanced features. For paid plans, Zoho starts at $14/user/month.

Is HubSpot free CRM enough for agencies?

HubSpot's free CRM works for agencies just starting out or with simple needs. It includes contact management, deal tracking, and email tracking. You'll need paid plans for marketing automation, custom reporting, and advanced features.

Should agencies pay annually or monthly for CRM?

Annual billing saves 10-20% on most platforms. Choose annual if you're confident in your CRM choice. Start monthly when trying a new platform, then switch to annual for the discount once committed.

Key Takeaways

When evaluating these tools, consider your specific needs:

  • Budget: Monthly costs and hidden fees
  • Integrations: Compatibility with your existing tools
  • Scalability: Ability to grow with your business
  • Support: Available help resources and response times
  • Security: Data protection and compliance features

Take advantage of free trials to test each tool thoroughly. The best choice depends on your unique workflow, team size, and growth plans.
