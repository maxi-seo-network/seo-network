Why Agencies Choose Pipedrive

Pipedrive was founded in 2010 by salespeople who wanted a CRM that actually worked for sales teams. Unlike HubSpot (marketing-first) or Salesforce (enterprise-first), Pipedrive is designed around the sales pipeline. For marketing agencies that prospect for clients—pitching proposals, managing deals, and closing retainers—Pipedrive's visual approach feels natural.

Pipedrive serves over 100,000 companies globally and is known for its intuitive interface. New users can be productive within hours, not weeks. For agencies without dedicated CRM administrators, this simplicity is a major advantage.

The trade-off? Pipedrive doesn't include marketing automation, content management, or client service features. If your agency needs all-in-one marketing + sales + service, HubSpot or Zoho CRM Plus are better choices. But for agencies primarily focused on pipeline management, Pipedrive offers the best value-to-functionality ratio in the market.

Pipedrive Features Built for Agency Workflows

Visual Pipeline Management

Pipedrive's signature feature is the drag-and-drop pipeline view. Each deal is a card that moves through stages: Lead In → Qualified → Proposal Sent → Negotiation → Won/Lost. Agencies can create multiple pipelines for different client types (retainer clients, project clients, referral partners) with custom stages for each. The visual approach makes pipeline status immediately clear during team meetings.

Deal Tracking for Retainers & Projects

Pipedrive handles both one-time projects and recurring retainers. Create deal templates with predefined stages for new client onboarding. Set up automatic deal creation when retainers renew. Track deal value (MRR for retainers, fixed fee for projects). The Professional tier adds product catalog, allowing agencies to bundle services into packages.

Email Integration & Tracking

Pipedrive syncs with Gmail and Outlook to log all client communications automatically. Every email to a contact is visible in their timeline. The Advanced tier includes email tracking (open/click notifications), which helps agencies know when clients have viewed proposals. Professional adds email sequences for automated follow-ups.

Activity Reminders & Automation

Pipedrive's activity system reminds account managers to follow up. Create activities (calls, emails, meetings) linked to deals. Set automated reminders. Use automation (Professional+) to create follow-up activities when deals enter certain stages or stall for too long. For agencies managing dozens of active prospects, this prevents leads from falling through cracks.

Custom Fields & Reporting

Professional tier adds custom fields and advanced reporting. Agencies can track client industry, project type, source, estimated close date, and any custom metric. Build dashboards showing deals by stage, win rate, average deal size, and sales cycle length. For agencies with structured sales processes, this data is invaluable for forecasting.

When Pipedrive Is Worth It for Agencies

  • You don't need marketing automation (use separate tools for that)
  • Your agency has 1-50 employees
  • You want CRM that works without extensive customization
  • Budget is $25-$50/user/month (vs. HubSpot's $50-$1,800+/month)
  • You use Gmail/Outlook and want seamless email integration

❌ Pipedrive is NOT right if:

  • You need marketing automation, email marketing, or landing pages
  • You want all-in-one marketing + sales + service platform
  • You need content management (blog, website, landing pages)
  • You require advanced workflow automation beyond simple triggers
  • You're an enterprise agency (100+ employees) with complex requirements
  • You need white-label client portals
  • You want a free CRM (HubSpot and Zoho have free tiers; Pipedrive does not)

Pipedrive vs. Alternatives for Marketing Agencies

Pipedrive vs. HubSpot

HubSpot costs $50/month (CRM Suite Starter) to $1,781+/month (Professional) and includes marketing automation, content management, and service hub. Pipedrive costs $24-$99/user/month for pure sales CRM. For agencies that already have marketing tools (ConvertKit, Mailchimp) and only need sales pipeline, Pipedrive is more cost-effective. For agencies wanting an all-in-one platform, HubSpot is worth the premium.

Pipedrive vs. Salesforce

Salesforce costs $25-$550/user/month and offers unlimited customization, enterprise security, and 3,000+ integrations. Pipedrive costs $24-$99/user/month and focuses on simplicity. Salesforce requires implementation partners ($15K-$60K) and weeks of training. Pipedrive can be set up in days without consultants. For agencies under $2M revenue, Pipedrive is usually sufficient. For agencies with complex requirements or enterprise clients, Salesforce offers more flexibility.

Pipedrive vs. Zoho CRM

Zoho CRM costs $14-$52/user/month and includes marketing automation, social media integration, and project management. Zoho's free tier (3 users) and low price make it attractive for budget-conscious agencies. Pipedrive's UX is generally considered superior, but Zoho offers more features per dollar. If you need marketing automation included, Zoho CRM Plus ($57/user/month) bundles CRM, marketing, and social tools for less than Pipedrive + separate marketing software.

Frequently Asked Questions About Pipedrive for Agencies

How much does Pipedrive cost for marketing agencies?

Pipedrive pricing starts at $24/user/month for Essential, $34/user/month for Advanced, $49/user/month for Professional, $64/user/month for Power, and $99/user/month for Enterprise (all billed annually). Most agencies use Professional ($49/user/month) for email automation and custom reporting. A 10-person agency pays $5,880/year on Professional.

Is Pipedrive good for marketing agencies?

Pipedrive is excellent for agencies focused on sales pipeline management and deal tracking. It's intuitive, affordable, and has a visual pipeline that's perfect for managing client prospects. However, Pipedrive lacks marketing automation, content management, and service features. Agencies needing all-in-one marketing+sales+service should consider HubSpot or Zoho CRM Plus.

Does Pipedrive have a free plan?

Pipedrive offers a 14-day free trial for all plans, but no permanent free tier. For agencies seeking free CRM options, HubSpot Free (up to 1,000 contacts) or Zoho CRM Free (up to 3 users) are better alternatives. Pipedrive requires paid subscription after the trial period.

What's the difference between Pipedrive Professional and Enterprise?

Pipedrive Professional ($49/user/month) includes email automation, custom reporting, and 15 pipeline stages. Enterprise ($99/user/month) adds dedicated account management, enhanced security (SSO, SCIM), advanced permissions, and unlimited customization. For most agencies, Professional provides sufficient functionality. Enterprise is worth it for agencies with 50+ users or strict security requirements.

Can Pipedrive handle retainers and recurring revenue?

Yes, Pipedrive can track recurring revenue through deal values and product catalogs. However, it's not designed specifically for subscription management. Use custom fields to track MRR, create deal templates for retainer renewals, and set up automated reminders for contract renewals. For sophisticated subscription management, integrate with tools like ProfitWell, Baremetrics, or Stripe.

Does Pipedrive integrate with agency tools?

Pipedrive integrates with 300+ apps via Pipedrive Marketplace, plus 4,000+ via Zapier. Common agency integrations include Slack, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, QuickBooks, Xero, Toggl, Harvest, Asana, Monday.com, and document signing tools. Professional tier adds API access for custom integrations.
