HubSpot vs Salesforce: The Great CRM Debate for Agencies

HubSpot and Salesforce dominate the CRM market, controlling over 35% combined market share. For marketing agencies, choosing between them isn't just about features—it's about philosophy. HubSpot offers an integrated "all-in-one" platform where marketing, sales, and service work together seamlessly. Salesforce provides a "configure anything" platform where you build exactly what you need, but pay more and invest heavily in implementation.

This isn't a sponsored comparison. We'll be transparent about where each platform excels and where they fall short. Both companies offer powerful tools, but they serve different agency profiles. The wrong choice can cost your agency $20,000-$100,000 in wasted implementation and missed productivity.

We'll compare pricing, features, implementation, total cost of ownership, and provide specific recommendations based on agency size and needs.

When to Choose HubSpot vs Salesforce

Choose HubSpot If Your Agency:

  • Generates under $2M annual revenue
  • Needs marketing automation integrated with CRM
  • Wants to avoid hiring a full-time CRM administrator
  • Prefers fast implementation (weeks, not months)
  • Values intuitive UX over maximum customization
  • Has a tech stack that integrates easily (HubSpot has 1,500+ native integrations)
  • Wants predictable pricing without hidden add-ons
  • Needs content management (blog, website, landing pages) included

Choose Salesforce If Your Agency:

  • Generates over $2M annual revenue
  • Needs unlimited customization and custom objects
  • Has complex workflows requiring advanced automation

  • Has 50+ users requiring granular permissions

  • Values flexibility and scalability over ease of use

Migration: Switching Between Platforms

HubSpot to Salesforce Migration

Agencies switch from HubSpot to Salesforce when they outgrow HubSpot's customization limits or need enterprise features. Process:

  1. Export contacts, companies, deals from HubSpot (CSV)
  2. Map HubSpot fields to Salesforce custom objects
  3. Recreate workflows and automation in Salesforce Flow
  4. Migrate content (HubSpot blog/landing pages → separate CMS)
  5. Set up Marketing Cloud for email automation
  6. Train team on Salesforce (8-40 hours per person)
  7. Run parallel systems for 2-4 weeks

Timeline: 3-6 months. Cost: $25,000-$75,000 with implementation partner.

Salesforce to HubSpot Migration

Agencies switch from Salesforce to HubSpot to reduce complexity, consolidate marketing+sales, and lower costs. Process:

  1. Use HubSpot's migration tool (free) or assisted migration ($3,000-$15,000)
  2. Import contacts, companies, deals, activities
  3. Recreate custom fields as HubSpot properties
  4. Set up workflows in HubSpot (simpler than Salesforce Flow)
  5. Migrate email templates and sequences
  6. Train team (2-8 hours per person)
  7. Run parallel for 1-2 weeks

Timeline: 1-3 months. Cost: $3,000-$20,000.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better for agencies: HubSpot or Salesforce?

HubSpot is better for agencies under $2M revenue that need integrated marketing, sales, and service tools. Salesforce is better for agencies over $2M revenue needing enterprise customization, compliance, or complex workflows. HubSpot is faster to implement and easier to use. Salesforce offers more flexibility and scalability.

Is HubSpot cheaper than Salesforce?

At entry level, yes—HubSpot offers a free tier while Salesforce's cheapest paid plan is $25/user/month. At scale, it depends. HubSpot CRM Suite Professional ($1,781/month for 5 users) costs roughly the same as Salesforce Enterprise for 10 users ($1,750/month). However, HubSpot includes marketing automation; Salesforce requires separate Marketing Cloud ($400+/month). Total cost of ownership often favors HubSpot for agencies needing marketing features.

Can I switch from Salesforce to HubSpot?

Yes, but plan for 2-3 months of migration. HubSpot offers migration tools for contacts, companies, deals, and activities. Custom objects and complex workflows need manual recreation. HubSpot's migration team charges $3,000-$15,000 depending on data complexity. Most agencies switch from Salesforce to HubSpot to reduce costs and simplify their tech stack.

Does HubSpot have better marketing features than Salesforce?

Yes. HubSpot's Marketing Hub ($20-$3,600/month) includes email marketing, landing pages, social media management, SEO tools, and marketing automation. Salesforce Marketing Cloud starts at ~$400/month and requires separate setup. For agencies, HubSpot's integrated marketing+sales+service is typically more practical than Salesforce's modular approach.

Which has better reporting: HubSpot or Salesforce?

Salesforce has more advanced reporting with Einstein Analytics (included in Unlimited, $550/user/month for Agentforce). HubSpot's reporting is sufficient for most agencies (custom dashboards, attribution reports, revenue tracking). If you need enterprise-grade analytics with custom data models, Salesforce wins. For standard agency reporting, HubSpot is fine.

Final Verdict

Agencies Under $1M Revenue

Choose HubSpot Free or Starter

HubSpot Free ($0) or Starter ($50/month) provides everything a small agency needs: contact management, email marketing, forms, live chat. No Salesforce tier offers equivalent value at this price point.

Agencies $1M-$2M Revenue

At $1,781/month with onboarding, HubSpot CRM Suite Professional delivers integrated marketing+sales+service for $30,000 first year. Salesforce Enterprise with Marketing Cloud would cost $50,000+ with implementation.

Agencies $2M+ Revenue

If you need compliance, custom objects, or enterprise integrations: Salesforce Enterprise/Unlimited. If you want integrated marketing with lower TCO: HubSpot Enterprise. Budget $60,000-$100,000+ for year one either way.

For 80% of agencies, HubSpot offers better value and faster implementation. Salesforce is the right choice only when you need its specific enterprise capabilities.
